### Finding: APP OPEN DROP (not funnel problem)

Comparing **Dec 2025 vs Feb 2026 M0 cohorts**:

**App Open % by Trial Engagement:**
• **T6+ (3+ watch days during trial):** 38.0% → 37.1% (**-2.2% decline**)
• T5-T7: 24.3% → 23.7% (-2.3%)
• T2-T4: 12.8% → 12.5% (-2.0%)
• T1: 6.3% → 5.9% (-5.1%)
• **T0 (0 watch days):** 1.8% → 1.3% (**-25.4% decline**)
• **Overall:** 16.6% → 16.1% (**-2.9% decline**)

---

### What This Means

1️⃣ **You were right about acquisition:**
The existence of T6+ tier (users who watched 3+ days during trial) with **38% app opens** proves acquisition brought engaged users. This is NOT "low quality."

2️⃣ **The problem is post-subscription:**
Users who engaged during trial are opening the app **2.2% less** post-subscription. This is NOT an acquisition problem — it's a **subscription-period engagement problem**.

3️⃣ **This is an app open drop, not a funnel issue:**
All tiers declined. If it were a funnel problem (app open → watch conversion), app opens would be stable. Instead, users aren't opening the app as frequently.

Two possibilities:

**A) Organic drop:**
Content library didn't match post-trial expectations → interest faded naturally

**B) Reengagement drop:**
Push notifications / emails / retargeting reduced between Dec → Feb
