## Core Beliefs & Philosophy

### On Entrepreneurship

> "No rational person, no sane person can pull through. You have to have a certain amount of irrational passion. And the word irrational is very important here."

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

HMT's flagship belief: **Entrepreneurship requires calibrated irrationality.** Rationality is disqualifying. You need passion that overrides rational risk assessment. This explains why he stayed after WittyFeed crashed — a rational person would've quit.

> "Entrepreneurship is one of the most soul-stirring journeys... it really makes you question about why are you doing what you're doing. And every day there are doubts being raised on the whys of everything that you do."

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

HMT frames entrepreneurship as **existential combat**, not business execution. The enemy is internal doubt, not market competition.

> "If you fall in love with the problem that you're going out to solve, then what happens is that until you solve the problem, actually, you are driven by the natural curiosity that you possess to solve that problem."

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

HMT's **mental model for resilience:** Problem-love > Solution-love. When the solution fails, most founders break because their identity was tied to it. HMT's identity is tied to the **question**, not the answer.

> "Another very important trait to see through these times is that you're just naturally curious. Like you have this almost impatient curiosity that is not going to get quenched until and unless you get to the answers, right?"

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

HMT's entrepreneurship = **applied curiosity**. He's building because **he needs to know the answer**, not to get rich or famous.

> "Entrepreneurial journeys are filled with paradoxes. They're filled with soul-stirring conundrums. Every day, you live in the gray. And ambiguity is a part of your life, right?"

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

HMT **doesn't seek certainty**. He's comfortable in ambiguity — sees it as the natural state of building, not a bug.

### On Identity & Growth

> "You are not professionally attached to any of your professional identities. You are a medium of getting things done."

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

HMT sees himself as **skill-agnostic, role-fluid**. This explains why he rejected "CPO" title ("doesn't justify everything I do"). His identity is **problem-solver**, not job title.

> "One of the things that I've imbibed through Ashoka... is the confidence that you can really unlearn anything and learn anything right... that one year at YF essentially really ingrained this in the fabric of my system that you are not professionally attached to any of your professional identities."

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

Ashoka = **meta-learning unlock**. Not "I learned economics" but "I learned how to learn anything." Explains his career pivots: robotics → EdTech → media → OTT.

### On Building Organizations

> "The organization is also an organism in itself that you end up creating. The culture speaks for it. It's also the act of creation, how you create the perception, how you create the product, how you create the people."

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

HMT sees the **company as creative output**, not just the product. Culture = creation. Perception = creation. People = creation.

> "It's very easy to talk about things like culture in abstraction. But it's super challenging to actually go out and implement it, because people are messy. And problems are messier."

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

HMT rejects **culture-as-poster-on-wall**. Culture is the emergent property of messy humans solving messy problems. You can't design it top-down.

> "As the company starts adding more and more people, it becomes increasingly exponentially tough to have all the people on the same page. And the culture starts getting morphed. And there's almost like a metamorphosis that happens with each new person that comes."

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

**Systems view of culture dilution:** Each hire = value add + cultural drift. You want the value, but it comes bundled with misalignment. This is **tension management**, not tension avoidance.

### On Ambiguity

> "At the end of the day, entrepreneurial journeys are filled with paradoxes. They're filled with soul-stirring conundrums. Every day, you live in the gray. And ambiguity is a part of your life, right? So, Ashoka taught me how to handle that."

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

HMT explicitly credits **Ashoka for his superpower** — ambiguity tolerance. This is THE skill for 0→1 building. No playbook, no right answer, constant unknowns.

> "One of the things that through the journey of entrepreneurship I personally believe that I have developed some expertise over is the idea of how to do the internal work to tackle ambiguity in your life."

**Source:** Ashoka Podcast, Feb 2026

HMT's unique expertise = **psychological resilience in uncertainty**. This is NOT a business course — it's a **self-regulation course**. He positions himself as a guide for internal work, not external tactics.
