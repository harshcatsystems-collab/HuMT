# 🧳 Hospital Bag Checklist

**Pack by:** Week 34-35 (July 2-9, 2026)
**Keep by:** Front door, ready to grab

---

## When to Pack

Don't wait until contractions start. Pack early, revise later.

- **Week 34:** Start packing non-essentials
- **Week 35:** Complete the bag
- **Week 36:** Do a final check
- **Week 37+:** Bag stays by the door

---

## 👩 For Divya (Mom)

### Documents (CRITICAL — Pack First)
- [ ] Hospital file / Medical records
- [ ] ID proof (Aadhaar, Passport)
- [ ] Health insurance card & papers
- [ ] Birth plan (if written)
- [ ] Emergency contact list
- [ ] Hospital pre-registration papers

### For Labor & Delivery
- [ ] Comfortable nightgown / hospital gown (front-opening for nursing)
- [ ] Warm socks (labor rooms are cold)
- [ ] Hair ties / headband
- [ ] Lip balm (hospitals are dry)
- [ ] Eyeglasses (if needed — skip contacts)
- [ ] Phone charger (long cord)
- [ ] Snacks for early labor (dry fruits, energy bars, biscuits)
- [ ] Water bottle
- [ ] Pillow from home (comfort matters)
- [ ] Music playlist / earphones

### For Recovery (Post-Delivery)
- [ ] 2-3 comfortable nightgowns (front-opening for nursing)
- [ ] Nursing bras (2-3)
- [ ] Maternity underwear (5-6, high-waisted, disposable ok)
- [ ] Maternity pads (hospital provides but bring extra)
- [ ] Breast pads
- [ ] Nipple cream (lanolin-based)
- [ ] Toiletries (toothbrush, toothpaste, face wash, moisturizer)
- [ ] Shower essentials (shampoo, soap, towel)
- [ ] Slippers / flip-flops
- [ ] Going-home outfit (loose, comfortable)
- [ ] Light blanket or shawl

### Nice to Have
- [ ] Massage oil for back
- [ ] Essential oil (lavender for calm)
- [ ] Kindle / book
- [ ] Tablet for entertainment
- [ ] Eye mask for rest
- [ ] Earplugs
- [ ] Small fan (hospitals can be warm)
- [ ] Mirror
- [ ] Light makeup (for photos if she wants)

---

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Pack in a bag with wheels** — you'll have your hands full
2. **Use packing cubes** — one for her, one for baby, one for you
3. **Pack a separate "labor bag"** — things you need immediately vs. recovery
4. **Bring an empty bag** — you'll leave with more than you came (hospital supplies, gifts)
5. **Take photos of bag contents** — in case you forget what's where
6. **Keep insurance/ID on top** — first thing they'll ask for
