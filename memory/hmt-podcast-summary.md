## 🎯 Core Themes

### 1. **Ashoka Shaped Him**
- "I embodied the core principles Ashoka imbues" (not an academic topper, but transformative year)
- Ashoka taught: **How to handle ambiguity and paradox** (essential for entrepreneurship)
- Liberal arts refined his entrepreneurial direction, gave breadth beyond technical skills
- **Credits Ashoka directly** for Stage having an ethnographic research department (anthropologists doing field studies)

### 2. **The Entrepreneurial Philosophy**

**What drives him:**
- **"Irrational passion"** — "No rational person can pull through"
- **Fall in love with the PROBLEM, not the solution** — sustains you until it's solved
- **Natural impatient curiosity** — won't stop until you get answers
- Entrepreneurship = "soul-stirring journey" filled with paradoxes, living in the gray

**On building courage:**
- Don't take leaps first — **take baby steps**
- College startups = validation + confidence building (not economic success, but learning fundamentals)
- "You have to accumulate courage until you have the threshold, then the question doesn't bother you"

**Key insight:**
> "Entrepreneurial journeys are filled with paradoxes, soul-stirring conundrums. Every day you live in the gray. Ambiguity is part of your life."

### 3. **The STAGE Origin Story (Detailed)**

**Not an OTT at first:**
- Started as **platform for artists** (tipping, monetization for creators)
- Founded Nov 1, 2019 (just celebrated 6 years)
- Evolved from WittyFeed insights: "People love content in their regional dialect"

**The founding question:**
> "Is there a dire need for people to feel validated, reinforced, and celebrated through the parts they identify with?"

**The realization:**
> "We are not building an entertainment company. We are building an **identity company**."

**First user feedback that changed everything:**
> "Thank you so much for saving our culture. We feel proud of our language when we watch Stage films."

### 4. **Three Myth-Breaking Learnings**

#### Myth 1: "India/Bharat doesn't pay for content"
**Reality:** People pay for premium, sensible, family-friendly content in their dialect.  
**Impact:** Stage broke this industry-wide belief.

#### Myth 2: "Dialect = rural = poor"
**Reality:** Language is about **identity, not class**.  
**Data:** 50% of Stage's 5M lifetime subs (2M active) are from Tier 1/2 cities.  
**Market penetration:** 15% of Haryana, single-digit in Rajasthan + Bhojpuri.

#### Myth 3: "Bharat is attention-led, not value-sensitive"
**Reality:** Stage monetizes through subscriptions, not just eyeballs.  
**Insight:** Previous companies treated Bharat as ad inventory. Stage proved they're premium customers.

### 5. **Ethnographic Research Department**

**Unique to Stage:** Anthropologists doing on-ground field studies (uncommon for OTT/startups).  
**Example shared:** 22-year-old girl in Bhojpuri village household using ChatGPT as companion, getting film recommendations. Her brother watches Avengers + Sherlock Holmes.  
**Takeaway:** "It's very easy to live in bubbles. Stage broke those bubbles."

### 6. **OTT++ Vision**

**What it means:**
- Beyond passive streaming → community building, EdTech, games, spiritual practices
- **Personalization** (1:1, not 1:many like cable TV)
- Regional-first but nationally scaled
- **"Not compromising on how it's tailored for Haryanvi vs Rajasthani"**

**Why it's possible:**
- Digital product = users spend time multiple times/day
- Opens opportunity for more than just streaming
- Example: Tipping for artists (early Stage feature)

**Status:** "It's a journey we're on. We'll get there sooner than later."

### 7. **Hardest Scale Challenges (Ranked)**

#### Easiest: **Tech** ✅
- Algorithms, concurrency, infrastructure — "more easily solved, standard practice"

#### Harder: **Brand & Marketing** ⚠️
- Keep repositioning as you move along the adoption curve (early adopters → early majority)
- Each segment = new user persona
- Constant reinvention needed

#### Harder: **Content Strategy** ⚠️
- Low shelf-life content = constant reconfiguration
- Serving 1M vs 2M = different strategies
- From individuals → families → households on TV
- Must cater to subgroups within households now

#### Hardest: **People & Culture** 🔥
- Small team = seamless alignment
- Scaling team = exponential misalignment
- **"People are messy, problems are messier, and it's the wetness of water"**
- Each hire brings value BUT also misalignment (which you want, but must manage)
- Culture in abstraction is easy; implementation is super challenging

**Quote:**
> "Culture starts getting morphed. There's a metamorphosis with each new person. They bring something new — that's why you hired them. But they also bring misalignment you have to solve for."

### 8. **On Burnout**

**Frequency:** "Almost every week" (not exaggeration — this is real)

**Why it's brutal:**
- Internal battles, not external wars
- Constant questioning of WHY you're doing this
- Doubts raised daily, beliefs challenged before they're validated
- **"Sinusoidal days"** — start high/low, wave through

**Quote:**
> "It's brutal. Not border-fighting brutal, but soul-stirring. It makes you question everything."

### 9. **The "Unlearn Anything, Learn Anything" Principle**

**From Ashoka/YIF:**
> "You are not professionally attached to any identity. You are a medium of getting things done."

**Example:** Never thought he'd be a film producer, but arts appreciation course gave him confidence to take on anything.

**Application:** This mindset lets him solve problems across domains (marketing, product, content, people).

### 10. **If HMT Taught at Ashoka**

**Course topic:** **"How to do the internal work to tackle ambiguity"**

**Why:**
- Not entrepreneurship/product/business (those are structured already)
- Unique to what HE can add value on
- **"Real hard need of the hour"** — world is becoming more ambiguous
- Will only increase from here
- **"Important to center yourself when facing ambiguous circumstances"**

**Who should take it:** Everybody (not cohort-specific)
