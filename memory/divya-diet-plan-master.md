## ⚠️ CRITICAL RULES (from Voice Calls)

### 1. Iron + Calcium = NEVER Together
Iron and calcium bind together — iron won't absorb if taken together.

| Situation | What to Do |
|-----------|------------|
| Dinner has paneer/dairy | Take iron supplement at **bedtime**, not with dinner |
| Morning calcium (milk) | Gap of **2 hours** before any iron-rich food |
| Taking iron supplement | Wait **2 hours** before milk |

**Real example:** Dr. Simran's client had very low hemoglobin despite consistent iron + calcium — turns out she was taking them 30 min apart. Iron wasn't absorbing. She ended up needing an IV drip.

### 2. Non-Veg Timing
- ❌ **NOT at dinner** — takes too long to digest, triggers acid reflux
- ✅ **5-6 PM snack** — this is the best time for chicken/fish
- Never post 6 PM

### 3. Dinner Target: 7:30-8:00 PM
- Early dinner helps manage fasting sugar spikes
- Sleep 3 hours after dinner
- Milk can come at bedtime

### 4. Tea Protocol
- Tea **30 minutes AFTER breakfast** — NOT with breakfast
- Better: Replace with turmeric + nutmeg milk
- Caffeine withdrawal lasts ~7-8 days (expect headaches)

## 🚫 FOODS TO RESTRICT

### Blood Sugar Control
| Restrict | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Refined carbs (sucrose, glucose, fructose, white rice, sago) | Spikes blood sugar |
| Table sugar, jaggery, sweets, honey, corn syrup | Spikes blood sugar |
| High-sugar fruits: banana, mango, custard apple, jackfruit, sapota, grapes | High natural sugars |
| Root vegetables in excess: potato, tapioca, sweet potato, yam, colocasia | Starchy |
| Fruit juices, sugarcane juice, sherbet | Sugar without fiber |
| Bakery: rusk, toast, bread, biscuits, cookies | Refined carbs |

### Foods to AVOID Completely
| Avoid | Replace With |
|-------|--------------|
| Processed foods (white bread, instant noodles, breakfast cereals) | Whole grains, brown rice, millets |
| Store-bought yogurt | Homemade curd |
| Carbonated beverages (sodas, colas, packaged juices) | Lemonade, Kokum sherbet, Buttermilk, Coconut water |
| Ready-to-eat breakfast cereals | Rolled oats, Dalia |
| Bakery products (rusk, khari, cookies, biscuits) | Roasted chana, pumpkin seeds |
| Desserts and sweets | Whole fruits, homemade milk custard, dark chocolate, homemade nut ladoo |

## 📋 BLOOD SUGAR CONTROL GUIDELINES

1. **Establish routine** — eat meals and snacks at regular times every day
2. **Never skip meals** — don't starve
3. **Choose healthier carbs** — whole grains, oats, millets, dried beans, legumes, peas, lentils
4. **Protein in every main meal** — dals, legumes, sprouts, paneer, beans
5. **Pair carbs with protein** — slows sugar absorption (e.g., whole wheat toast + peanut butter)
6. **Include healthy fats** — avocados, almonds, walnuts, chia, flax, olive oil, ghee
7. **Only 2 medium fruits/day** — preferably in first half, NOT with main meals
8. **Portion control** — use smaller plates, limit starchy foods
9. **High fiber (25-30g/day)** — spinach, okra, fruits with peels
10. **Stay hydrated** — water regulates blood sugar, prevents unnecessary snacking
