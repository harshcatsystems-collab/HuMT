# 📚 Newborn Prep — Knowledge & Skills

**For:** HMT (Dad-to-be)
**Purpose:** Learn before the panic. Know enough to be confident, not anxious.

---

## The Dad Mindset

You don't need to know everything. You need to know:
1. How to keep baby safe
2. How to support Divya
3. When to ask for help
4. That it's okay to figure it out as you go

Every parent learns on the job. You will too.

## 🍼 Essential Skills to Learn BEFORE Baby

### 1. Diaper Changing

**How long to learn:** 5 minutes of watching, 10 diapers of practice

**Steps:**
1. Lay baby on back on changing surface
2. Open dirty diaper but don't remove yet (catches surprises)
3. Lift baby's legs gently by ankles
4. Wipe front to back (especially for girls)
5. Slide dirty diaper out
6. Slide clean diaper under (tabs at back)
7. Bring front up between legs
8. Fasten tabs snugly but not tight (two fingers should fit)
9. Done!

**Pro tips:**
- Have everything ready BEFORE starting
- Keep a hand on baby at all times on elevated surfaces
- Boys spray — open diaper, wait 5 seconds, then proceed
- Diaper cream only if rash, not every time

**Watch:** Search "how to change newborn diaper" — any video works

---

### 2. Swaddling

**Why:** Mimics womb, reduces startle reflex, helps sleep

**Steps (Diamond method):**
1. Lay blanket in diamond shape, fold top corner down
2. Place baby with shoulders at fold line
3. Straighten left arm, wrap left side across body, tuck under
4. Fold bottom up over feet
5. Straighten right arm, wrap right side across, tuck under
6. Should be snug around chest, loose around hips

**Pro tips:**
- Snug enough they can't wiggle out
- Loose around hips (prevents hip dysplasia)
- Stop swaddling when baby can roll (~2-3 months)

**Watch:** "How to swaddle a baby" — plenty of tutorials

---

### 3. Holding a Newborn

**Key principle:** Always support the head. Their neck muscles are weak.

**Positions:**
- **Cradle hold:** Head in crook of elbow, body along forearm
- **Football hold:** Head in hand, body along forearm tucked at your side
- **Shoulder hold:** Baby upright against your shoulder, one hand under butt, one supporting head/neck
- **Face-down hold:** Baby face-down along your forearm (good for gas)

**Pro tip:** Babies are tougher than they look. Be gentle but don't be terrified.

---

### 4. Burping

**Why:** Babies swallow air while feeding. Burping prevents gas pain.

**When:** After every 2-3 oz of bottle, or when switching breasts, and at end of feed

**Methods:**
1. **Over shoulder:** Baby upright on your shoulder, pat/rub back
2. **Sitting up:** Sit baby on your lap, support chin with one hand, pat back with other
3. **Face-down on lap:** Baby across your thighs face-down, pat back

**Pro tips:**
- Firm pats > gentle pats
- Put a burp cloth down — spit-up happens
- No burp after 5 mins? They might not need one

---

### 5. Feeding Support (For Divya)

**If breastfeeding:**
- Bring her water every single time she feeds
- Bring snacks (nursing makes you hungry)
- Help position pillows
- Handle diaper changes so she can rest after
- Don't give advice unless asked — it's harder than it looks

**Signs of good latch:**
- Baby's mouth wide open (like a yawn)
- Lips flanged out, not tucked in
- Chin touching breast
- Audible swallowing

**Warning signs (tell doctor):**
- Pain that doesn't improve after first minute
- Cracked, bleeding nipples
- Baby not gaining weight
- Fewer than 6 wet diapers/day after Day 4

**If bottle-feeding:**
- Pace the feeding (don't let them guzzle)
- Hold bottle horizontal, not tilted up
- Burp frequently
- Never prop a bottle and leave

### 6. Bathing

**When to start:** Sponge baths until umbilical cord falls off (~1-2 weeks), then tub baths

**Frequency:** 2-3 times a week is enough. Over-bathing dries skin.

**Sponge bath steps:**
1. Gather everything first (warm room, warm water, towel, washcloth, mild soap)
2. Keep baby wrapped, expose only part you're washing
3. Wipe face with damp cloth (no soap)
4. Wash scalp gently
5. Clean body, creases, behind ears
6. Pat dry thoroughly

**Tub bath steps:**
1. Fill tub with 2-3 inches warm water (test with elbow — should feel warm, not hot)
2. Support head and neck with your forearm, hand gripping far armpit
3. Ease baby in feet first
4. Wash with other hand
5. Lift out, wrap immediately — they get cold fast

### 7. Soothing a Crying Baby

**The 5 S's (Dr. Harvey Karp):**
1. **Swaddle** — Snug wrap
2. **Side/Stomach** — Hold on side or stomach (for soothing, not sleep)
3. **Shush** — Loud shushing sound (louder than you think)
4. **Swing** — Rhythmic jiggling motion
5. **Suck** — Pacifier or finger to suck on

**Crying checklist:**
- Hungry?
- Wet/dirty diaper?
- Tired?
- Too hot/cold?
- Gas/needs burp?
- Overstimulated?
- Sick? (check temp)
- Just needs to be held?

**When to call doctor:**
- Fever (>100.4°F / 38°C) in newborn
- Not eating
- Unusual cry (high-pitched, weak)
- Lethargy
- Difficulty breathing

---

### 8. Safe Sleep

**ABCs of safe sleep:**
- **A**lone — No co-sleeping with adults (room-sharing OK, bed-sharing not recommended)
- **B**ack — Always on back for sleep
- **C**rib — Firm mattress, fitted sheet, nothing else

**Never:**
- Put baby to sleep on stomach or side
- Use pillows, blankets, bumpers in crib
- Let baby sleep on couch, armchair, or your chest (if you might fall asleep)
- Overheat the room

## 🆘 Emergency Knowledge

### Infant CPR (Learn This)

**When:** Baby unresponsive and not breathing normally

**Steps:**
1. Check responsiveness — tap foot, call name
2. Call for help (shout, have someone call 102/ambulance)
3. 30 chest compressions — two fingers on breastbone, push 1.5 inches deep, fast
4. 2 rescue breaths — cover mouth AND nose, gentle puff (chest should rise)
5. Repeat until help arrives or baby responds

**Watch:** Search "infant CPR" — American Heart Association videos are best

**Take a class:** Many hospitals offer infant CPR courses. Worth the 2 hours.

---

### Choking (Learn This)

**Signs:** Can't cry/cough, turning blue, silent

**For infant (<1 year):**
1. 5 back blows — Hold face-down on forearm, support head, 5 firm slaps between shoulder blades
2. 5 chest thrusts — Turn face-up, two fingers on breastbone, 5 quick thrusts
3. Repeat until object comes out or baby cries/coughs/breathes

**Watch:** "Infant choking first aid" — know this before baby arrives

## 👨‍👩‍👦 Supporting Divya

### First 2 Weeks (Survival Mode)

**Your job:**
- Let her focus on feeding and resting
- Handle EVERYTHING else — diapers, meals, laundry, visitors
- Bring water/snacks every time she feeds
- Take night shifts when possible (even if just burping/changing)
- Tell her she's doing amazing (she won't feel like it)

**Watch for postpartum warning signs:**
- Extreme sadness lasting more than 2 weeks
- Inability to care for baby or self
- Thoughts of harming self or baby
- Not sleeping even when baby sleeps
- Withdrawal, not eating

*Postpartum depression is common and treatable. Don't ignore signs. Talk to doctor.*

### Your Own Mental Health

- It's okay to feel overwhelmed
- It's okay to not feel instant "magical" bond
- It's okay to miss your old life
- It's okay to ask for help

*You're not weak. You're human.*
