# HMT Writing Voice Profile

*Reference for drafting emails, updates, and documents in HMT's voice.*
*Built from observed patterns across Slack, email, and meeting transcripts.*

---

## Tone & Style

- **Direct, no fluff.** Gets to the point fast. No "I hope this email finds you well."
- **Lowercase casual** in Slack/internal comms. Proper case for external/investor emails.
- **Short sentences.** Fragments are fine. "Approved." "Let's do this." "Not yet."
- **Questions as nudges.** "can we get this by eod?" not "Would it be possible to..."
- **Warm but efficient** with team. Not cold, but doesn't pad with pleasantries.
- **Strategic framing** for investors/board — always ties back to business impact.
- **Hindi/Hinglish sprinkles** in internal comms naturally. "theek hai", "chalo", "accha".

## Email Patterns

### Internal (team, DRs)
- Subject: short, action-oriented ("re: ML widget results", "need input on Manasvi")
- Body: 2-3 lines max. Often just a question or directive.
- Sign-off: usually none, or just "H" or "- harsh"

### External (investors, partners)
- More structured but still concise
- Opens with context, not pleasantries
- Clear ask or update
- Sign-off: "Best, Harsh" or "Thanks, Harsh"

### Co-founders (Vinay, Parveen, Shashank)
- Ultra-casual. No subject line formality needed.
- Often just shares a link or screenshot with 1-line context.

## Slack Patterns
- Lowercase almost always
- Heavy use of "approved", "lets do this", "check with [name]"
- Reacts more than replies when possible (thumbsup, eyes)
- Delegates clearly: "@name can you handle this"

## Decision Communication
- States decision + brief rationale
- Doesn't over-explain
- "Going with option B — better unit economics and we can ship this week."

## When Saying No
- Kind but clear: "not right now — let's revisit after Q1 close"
- Never harsh, but never ambiguous

---

## Usage

When drafting for HMT:
1. Match the context (internal vs external vs investor)
2. Keep it SHORT — if you can say it in 2 lines, don't use 5
3. Lead with the decision or ask
4. Include data when available — HMT backs things with numbers
5. Always flag as DRAFT for HMT's review before sending
