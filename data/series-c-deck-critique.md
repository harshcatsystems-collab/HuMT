## Executive Summary

The deck is polished, confident, and in classic HMT style — big thinking, bold claims, unapologetic about the vision. But there are credibility gaps when cross-referenced with operational data.

**Key Risks:**
1. Retention narrative conflicts with dormant subscriber data
2. AI cost claims may be unsubstantiated  
3. Aggressive growth timeline (14 markets in 12 months)
4. Unit economics gap (CAC ₹526 vs deck's ~₹400)

**Score:** 7.5/10 — Fix retention framing before investor meetings

---

## 1. Thesis (Slides 1-3)

### What Works
- Three-thesis framework is clean and memorable
- "Cultural entertainment is structurally underserved" — strongest thesis
- AI complements cultural (AI enables scale of cultural content)

### Needs Nuance
- **Tension:** If AI makes it easy, why can't competitors replicate? Which is the moat — cultural insight or AI pipeline? The deck does both, but worth being explicit.
- Slide 3 ("We are the only team that can execute") is bold but circular — "culturally-driven founders" is assertion, not evidence.

### Investor Pushback
> "If the market is structurally underserved, why is retention bad?"

### Suggestion
Add: "AI makes replication possible; cultural context makes it authentic."

## 3. Retention (Slides 6-7) — 🚨 BIGGEST GAP

### What Works
- M0 40% retention genuinely strong for India
- Long series (90% WoW return), micro dramas (2.3x D30) — good format innovations
- "Structural Retention, not hit-driven" differentiates from Netflix/Disney

### 🚨 The Problem
- **1.1M dormant subscribers** (paying but NOT watching) — not addressed in deck
- Active base shrinking: 1.82M (Jan) → 1.70M (Feb) = -6.5%
- Monthly watch: 25% vs Netflix 40% — highlights weakness

### Investor Pushback
> "You claim 'habit-forming content' with 40% M0 retention but 1.1M dormant. Explain."

### Suggestion
Frame dormant as reactivation opportunity, not failure. 1.1M = future upside.
