# The Dormant Problem: STAGE's Subscriber Identity Crisis

**For:** HMT, Co-Founder — STAGE OTT

**Author:** OpenClaw Analysis

## Deep Reflection

### The Ratio That Should Terrify Us

1.1M dormant subscribers. ~87K active watchers. That's a **13:1 ratio** — for every person watching STAGE, thirteen people are "subscribers" who aren't watching.

This isn't an engagement problem. This is a **subscriber definition problem.**

### The Two Populations Are Two Completely Different Problems

**Never Watched (676K):** These people subscribed and never opened the app to watch content. They are not dormant users — they are **non-users who happen to have a subscription.** Most of the 375K LVUs in this pool are almost certainly carrier billing acquisitions who don't know they're subscribed, don't care, or both. They're ghosts in a spreadsheet.

**Previously Watched (441K):** These people found STAGE, watched content, and stopped. They are the real dormant pool — people who demonstrated intent and churned from engagement. This is where resurrection ROI lives.

### The M0 Pipeline to Dormancy

The M0 data makes this story airtight: **65% of M0 subscribers (110K) have zero activity.** That's where the never-watched pool comes from. Every month, ~5K new subscribers per day arrive, 65% never engage, and they flow directly into the never-watched dormant pool. The never-watched HVU pool grew 30% (148K → 192K) — acquisition is actively making the problem worse.

The pipeline: Carrier billing / growth campaign → Subscribe → Never open app → Never-watched dormant → Permanently lost

### The Feb 17 Spike: Something Worked

On Feb 17, resurrection spiked **2x across all segments:**
- Previously-watched HVU: 0.56% → **1.03%** (nearly doubled)
- Previously-watched MVU: 0.35% → **0.48%**
- Never-watched conversions also lifted

This wasn't random noise — it hit every segment simultaneously. Possible causes:
1. **Content release** — a major title dropped that pulled people back
2. **Push notification campaign** — a well-targeted blast
3. **External event** — something cultural (regional holiday, cricket match, viral moment)

**HMT needs to find out what happened on Feb 17 and systematize it.** If a single intervention can 2x resurrection for one day, doing it weekly would transform the active base.

### The Math: What's Actually Possible

**Conservative scenario — 5% monthly resurrection of previously-watched HVUs:**
- Pool: 107K HVUs × 5% = **5,350 reactivated users/month**
- Current: ~860 HVU resurrections/month (0.8% × 107K ÷ 30 × 30... ~25,700/month actually, but many don't stick)
- If even half stick for a week, that's meaningful WAU lift

**Aggressive scenario — 5x current resurrection rate (board deck showed CLM can do +143-180%):**
- Current daily: 3,140 resurrections → **15,700/day**
- Monthly unique: ~47K → could mean +30-50K WAU if half retain for a week
- Against current ~87K active watchers, that's a **35-57% increase in active base**

The board deck proved CLM resurrection went from 20.3K/month to 49.3K/month for previously-watched (+143%). There's still room. The question isn't whether resurrection works — it's whether STAGE is investing enough in it relative to new acquisition.

### The LVU Graveyard: Stop Counting Ghosts

375K never-watched LVUs. 0.26% daily conversion. These are overwhelmingly carrier billing ghosts — people who were subscribed through telco bundles and have zero intent. Keeping them in the subscriber count:
1. **Inflates subscriber metrics** — making the business look bigger than it is
2. **Dilutes engagement rates** — MAU%, conversion%, retention% all look worse
3. **Wastes CLM resources** — any targeting of this pool has near-zero ROI

**Position: STAGE should create a "Qualified Subscriber" metric that excludes never-watched LVUs older than 90 days.** Report both numbers to the board, but operate on qualified subscribers internally.

### The Subscriber Quality Question

Here's the uncomfortable truth: STAGE reports ~1.58M subscribers. But:
- 676K never watched anything (43%)
- 441K watched before but stopped (28%)
- ~87K are active watchers (5.5%)
- The rest are in various M0/transitional states

**Only ~5.5% of "subscribers" are actively watching.** In any other business, this would be called a vanity metric. The 1.58M number is useful for fundraising and board decks. It's misleading for operational decision-making.

**My recommendation: Redefine "active subscriber" as someone who has watched at least once in the last 30 days.** This would put STAGE's real subscriber count at ~250-300K — still impressive for a dialect OTT, and far more honest.

## The Subscriber Quality Question

Should STAGE redefine "subscriber"?

**Yes.** Here's why:

The current definition (anyone with an active subscription) includes 676K people who have never watched anything. This:
- Inflates metrics for investors and board (temporarily useful, eventually trust-destroying)
- Dilutes all engagement KPIs (MAU%, retention%, watch conversion%)
- Misallocates CLM resources toward unwinnable segments
- Creates a false sense of scale

**Proposed taxonomy:**
| Tier | Definition | Est. Size |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Active Subscriber** | Watched in last 30 days | ~250-300K |
| **Dormant Subscriber** | Previously watched, inactive >30 days | ~441K |
| **Unactivated Subscriber** | Never watched, subscription active | ~676K |
| **Ghost Subscriber** | Never watched LVU, >90 days dormant | ~300K |

Report total subscribers for market positioning. Operate on active + dormant subscribers for product and CLM decisions. Ignore ghosts.
