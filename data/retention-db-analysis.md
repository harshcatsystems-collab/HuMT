# Weekly Watch Retention: The Declining Curve
## Executive Analysis — Feb 23, 2026

**For:** HMT (Co-Founder, STAGE OTT)
**Data Sources:** Active Subscription Watch Retention DB (#1490), M0 Engagement Dashboard (#2182), Board Deck (Jan 2026)

---

## Executive Summary

**STAGE's weekly watch retention has declined from 50.3% to 40.8% over 6 weeks (Dec 29 → Feb 9), with a partial recovery to 44.5% (Feb 16).** Simultaneously, the active watcher base has eroded from 141K to 87K — a 38% contraction. These trends are compounding: fewer watchers AND a smaller share returning each week.

This is happening against the backdrop of a Jan board deck that showed STAGE at #3 in India for M1 retention (53%). The board story was "we're winning." The live dashboard says "we're leaking."

**These are not contradictory.** The board deck measured monthly retention of *all* subscribers. This dashboard measures *weekly* retention of *active watchers* — a narrower, more demanding metric. Both can be true: STAGE activates well at M1, but the weekly watching habit is eroding for users past the novelty phase.

The alarm is not that retention dropped. It's that **core habitual users (Repeat 2)** are declining — from 61% to 50% — and the mid-funnel subscription stages (D_15to60) are collapsing. This is structural, not seasonal.

- The watcher base began contracting sharply: 141K → 116K in 2 weeks

**The Feb 16 recovery** (44.5%) may reflect base compression: as casual watchers churn out, the remaining 87K skew more engaged, inflating the percentage. This is survivorship bias, not genuine improvement.

## 2. The Two Cliffs

### Cliff 1: Week 3-4 (D_15to30)
- Dec 29: **45.1%** → Feb 16: **37.2%** (−8pp)
- Users who survived onboarding and formed a 2-week pattern start dropping off
- This is the **content discovery cliff**: initial recommendations exhausted, user hasn't found a "home show"
- Aligns with M0 data: only 4.66% achieve habit moment. The other 95% hit this wall.

### Cliff 2: Month 2-3 (D_31to60)
- Dec 29: **45.3%** → Feb 9: **30.2%** (−15pp)
- The steepest decline of any segment — nearly halved
- This is the **subscription regret cliff**: user has been paying for 1-2 months, hasn't found enough value
- Directly maps to the board deck's M1→M3 drop (#3 at M1, #9 at M3)
- These users are the ones deciding whether to renew. If they're not watching, they won't.

**Together, these cliffs explain the watcher base erosion.** New subscribers enter at D_0to7 with ~45% retention, but bleed through D_15to60 at 31-37%, and many never make it to renewal.

## 4. The Repeat 2 Alarm

Repeat 2 users (watched 2+ consecutive weeks) are the core habitual base. Their decline is the most concerning signal in this entire dashboard:

- Jan 5: **61.0%** → Feb 2: **50.3%** → Feb 16: **55.3%**

An 11pp decline in habitual user retention means **the product experience is degrading for your best users.** This cannot be explained by acquisition mix or seasonal effects. Possible causes:

1. **Content fatigue** — Core users have consumed the best titles. New releases aren't replacing at the rate of consumption.
2. **Algorithmic staleness** — Recommendations cycling the same content to repeat visitors.
3. **Mahapunarjanam effect** — The board deck showed this series driving 40% weekly returns through 12 weeks. If the season ended or episode gaps occurred, Repeat 2 users lose their anchor show.
4. **Quality regression** — New content releases not meeting the bar set by breakout titles.

---

## 5. Watcher Base Erosion: 141K → 87K

The 38% contraction in 5 weeks is the compounding problem. Even if retention rates stabilize at 44%, each week applies that rate to a smaller base:

- Week 1: 141K × 48% = 67.7K retained
- Week 5: 87K × 44% = 38.3K retained

**The funnel is narrowing at both ends.** Fewer new watchers entering (M0 watch conversion stuck at 3.09%) AND fewer existing watchers returning. Without reversing the base contraction, retention rate improvements are cosmetic.

**Where did the 54K watchers go?**
- Some churned from subscription entirely
- Some remain subscribed but stopped watching (joined the 1.17M dormant pool)
- Some are in resurrection windows (Resurrect 1-4 segments total 30.6K watchers in Feb 16 data)

## 7. Renewal Users: The Backbone

Renewal users (52.7K of 87.3K = **60% of all watchers**) retain at 48.4% — the highest rate. They're the bedrock.

**Risks:**
- Even renewals dropped from 52.8% → 42.6% before recovering to 48.4%
- If the content slate weakens, renewal users will notice first — they've seen everything
- Renewal users staying = survivorship. The question is whether the *next* cohort to reach renewal age will be as strong, given D_31to60 is at 31.6%

**Protection strategy:**
- Ensure weekly release cadence for long-format series (their primary consumption mode)
- TV app push (renewal users are the most likely TV adopters, and TV = 2.4x D30 retention)
- Cross-format exposure: board deck showed 48% M0 retention for dual-format users vs 40% single-format

## 8. The 2-Week Resurrection Window

| Gap Length | Retention | Implication |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Resurrect 1 (1 week) | 41.5% | Recoverable — intervene NOW |
| Resurrect 2 (2 weeks) | 35.5% | Still viable — last best chance |
| Resurrect 3 (3 weeks) | 30.8% | Diminishing returns |
| Resurrect 4+ (4+ weeks) | 33.6% | Mostly lost |

**After 2 weeks of inactivity, recovery probability drops below 35% and doesn't meaningfully recover.** CLM interventions (push notifications, OB calls, content nudges) must fire within the first 14 days of inactivity to have impact.

Current CLM framework segments by 1-week, 2-3 week, and 4+ week inactive. The data suggests the **most aggressive intervention should happen in the first 7 days** (Resurrect 1 at 41.5% vs Resurrect 2 at 35.5% — a 6pp gap for one week of delay).

## 9. Strategic Recommendations

### P0 — Do This Week

1. **Content calendar audit.** Map weekly release dates against the Jan 26 retention inflection. Identify any gap in new episode drops. If Mahapunarjanam S1 ended without a replacement, that's likely the trigger.

2. **Resurrect 1 intervention acceleration.** Move CLM triggers for 1-week inactive users from current cadence to day 1 of inactivity. Every day of delay costs recovery probability.

3. **D_31to60 emergency deep-dive.** This segment collapsed 15pp. Pull user-level data: what did they watch? When did they stop? What was the last content consumed? Find the pattern.

### P1 — This Sprint (2 Weeks)

4. **Cross-format exposure in M0.** Board deck proved 8pp M0 lift for dual-format users. Ensure every new subscriber sees both microdrama and long-format within first session. Product/algo change.

5. **Bhojpuri content acceleration.** 67.5K M0 subs (largest dialect) at 2.51% watch conversion vs 3.5% for others. Only 23 microdramas vs 44 Haryanvi. Content-market fit isn't there yet.

6. **Renewal user TV push.** 60% of watcher base is renewals. TV users show 2.4x D30 retention. Targeted campaign: "Watch on TV" for renewal users. This protects the backbone.

### P2 — This Month

7. **Algorithmic freshness overhaul.** Core Repeat 2 users declining suggests stale recommendations. Implement diversity injection — ensure repeat visitors see new/unseen content in top slots.

8. **Weekly content velocity target.** Set a minimum: X new episodes/week across all dialects. Track as a north star alongside retention. Content drought = retention drought.

9. **"Home show" matching.** The D_15to30 cliff is a content discovery failure. Users need a show that becomes "theirs" by week 3. Test: assign a recommended series prominently on return visit, with progress tracking and "next episode" nudges.

10. **Watcher base recovery target.** Set explicit weekly goal: reverse the contraction. Target 100K watchers by end of March. Track inflow (new + resurrected) vs outflow (lapsed) weekly.

## Bottom Line

STAGE built something real — #3 M1 retention in India, proven CLM framework, strong content-market fit. But the live data shows the gains are leaking. The mid-funnel (weeks 3-8) is collapsing, core users are degrading, and the watcher base is shrinking.

This isn't a crisis yet. Renewal users are holding. The structural improvements from 2025 (CLM, micro-drama, TV, long-format series) are intact. But without immediate action on content velocity, mid-funnel engagement, and resurrection timing, the 50% → 41% slide will continue — and at 87K watchers, there's less margin for error than there was at 141K.

**The next 4 weeks will determine whether Feb was a seasonal dip or the start of a structural decline.**
