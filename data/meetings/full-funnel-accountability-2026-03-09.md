Summary
Parveen Singhal led a discussion to define clear roles and accountabilities for various metrics across the acquisition, trial, and retention phases of the user funnel, assigning Shubham to oversee the entire acquisition piece, including growth and traffic, and Nikl to manage the trial and retention metrics such as moment trial consumption metric (TCR) and trial retention. Critical business metrics like Subscription Rate (SR), which Parveen Singhal noted is a revenue metric driven by lower TCR and better watch retention, require separate ownership for maximization; the team suggested a model where Shubham has complete ownership over their piece, with others assisting. Furthermore, the team decided to set a two-week window for addressing current issues and rethinking the design approach, which includes defining the strategy for reacquisition and reactivation, with a potential split of responsibilities between Alia (reactivation) and Jagrati (reacquisition).

Details
Defining Roles and Accountability: Parveen Singhal initiated the discussion by emphasizing the need to define individual roles and accountabilities for various matrices, stating that "everyone is a builder" and that roles need clear definitions of "who does what". They stressed that this process starts with content strategy, currently operating under a format strategy for alignment. The discussion began with acquisition and re-engagement, aligning the strategy from the top until the last funnel stage.
Acquisition and Growth Accountability: The meeting participants identified that feature films are heavy acquisition drivers, alongside "Contank videos," and that "solder content" is considered more important than "real content" for market acquisition. Shubham was proposed to be accountable for the entire acquisition piece, which covers growth, reach, and traffic, noting that these elements constitute the acquisition phase of the funnel.
Trial and Retention Metrics Ownership: Critical activations, including moment trial consumption metric (TCR) and trial retention, were identified as one piece of the funnel, with the end goal being the improvement of subscription rate (SR). Nikl was assigned to handle this piece, focusing on activation, recognizing that they have already been involved in this work. Parveen Singhal noted that SR is primarily a revenue metric and an output, driven by lower TCRs and better watch retention.
Subscription Rate (SR) and Activation Alignment: There was an extensive discussion about the relationship between SR, TCR, and watch retention, with the understanding that SR will cause action and that both TCR and watch retention lead to a higher SR. It was clarified that quality of acquisition impacts subscription quality, though this piece does not solely determine quality based only on revenue but also on watching metrics. Parveen Singhal maintained that while there is dependency and correlation among metrics, the accountability for SR cannot be eliminated.
Optimal SR Ownership: The team discussed the influence of TCR on SR, acknowledging that TCR currently accounts for a significant portion of SR impact due to the feature film format. Despite this, the consensus was that SR needs maximization, and ownership of SR should remain a separate accountability because it is a critical revenue metric. Parveen Singhal suggested a model where Shubham has complete ownership and accountability for their piece, with others providing assistance and collaboration.
Reacquisition and Reactivation Strategy: The group considered the strategy for reacquisition and reactivation, noting that these are fundamentally different concepts. It was hypothesized that different people should be assigned to these two areas, suggesting that Alia could focus on reactivation and Jagrati on reacquisition. Parveen Singhal reinforced the idea that people, such as those in content working on re-engagement, should work closely with the retention team.
Metric Ownership and Future Planning: A key decision was made that at the metric ownership level, only one person should own one metric. Parveen Singhal emphasized moving into a "preemptive design mode" where ownership is clear, even if it requires cross-functional efforts. They concluded that the distinction between reactivation (focusing on trial takers) and reacquisition (focusing on non-trial-takers) is critical, and a follow-up meeting is needed to finalize better execution plans.
Renewal Rate and Metric Decoupling: The team discussed renewal rate as a critical business metric, with a working assumption that increasing watching might improve retention in terms of renewal. Parveen Singhal proposed that the metrics should be decoupled, suggesting that the SR and TCR focus should be a separate revenue priority from increasing the number of users who watch. They stated that the business needs to increase users who pay and those who watch, suggesting that separate optimization is necessary.
Coach Retention and Design Rethinking: Parveen Singhal stressed that Coach Retention is fundamentally the first and most important thing to solve to avoid ending up in the same negative situation. They proposed the need to rethink the design approach based on a fundamental shift in thinking. A two-week window was established to review the current situation, after which the team should discuss the realities and decide whether to iterate the design or not.
Freezing the Plan and Accountability: The team decided to create a two-week window for resolving the issues, barring direct selling via subscription. Following this period, they plan to freeze the model and plan, and then move to execution. Parveen Singhal emphasized the need to align properly on accountability, review the current thinking, question the existing model, and address any gaps in planning or required re-accountability setting.
Future Meeting Focus and Strategic Debates: The next meeting will focus on three key matrices to include, determining the correct order for those matrices, and debating these items. It was suggested that solving current problems might require personnel from different teams and that the focus should be on a "full funnel first" approach. Parveen Singhal also mentioned the upcoming Series C fundraising campaign launch and the plan to raise venture debt to address immediate cash flow issues.
Financial Strategy and Investor Expectations: The venture debt being raised is intended to solve cash flow problems for six months until new money is secured. Parveen Singhal stated that the expectation from investors is centered on profitability growth and retention, with a goal of month-on-month revenue growth. Every decision made must be guided by the fundamental thinking that the current cash flow problem needs to be solved for the next nine months.
Regional Content Strategy and Execution: The decision to launch content in Punjabi, Bengali, Assami, Odia, Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, and Malayalam has been made, and the execution plan will be created. Content in Hindi is still under discussion and will be addressed properly to reach a final answer. It was noted that while regional content might have less reach, it has been observed to have high revenue per acquired user.
