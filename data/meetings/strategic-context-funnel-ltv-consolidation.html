Part 2: LTV-Centric Thinking (LTV Optimization — Mar 9 AM)

Fundamental Shift in Thinking

OLD MODEL: Content factory where all acquisition is equal
NEW MODEL: Each content is a unique product with specific business outcomes

Three Core Business Outcomes (Content-Driven)

  1. Acquire — bring new users
  2. Re-engage — activate dormant subscribers
  3. Reacquire — bring back churned users

Critical insight: Content format determines which outcome it solves

Content Strategy Realities

Feature films (single-serve):
- "Dead content" for TCR
- Lower Trial Conversion Rate
- Can confuse users on what platform offers

Binge-worthy content (series):
- Higher TCR
- Builds habit moments
- Drives frequency

Key principle: "Content is the core reality" — everything else (promo, pricing, activation) layers on top

Content Performance Metrics

Two main metrics:
1. Watch Rate (WTR) — internal distribution metric (% of cohort that starts watching)
2. Completion Rate (CR) — quality marker (depth of engagement)

Supporting metrics:
- Active watch days (frequency)
- Watch timelessness (depth in watch cycle)

NOT a metric: Potential frequency (it's a creative output, not business outcome)

TCR and SR Controllability

Can be influenced BEFORE trial starts:
- Promotional content creates intent
- How you position content drives trial quality
- Example: Promoting 8-min episodes as "family watch" increased TCR

Key insight: Promo strategy is as important as content itself

The Dormant Subscriber Crisis

1.1M dormant subscribers — paying but not watching
Described as: "Biggest pain in the ass for sale right now"
Problem: Team was too focused elsewhere to solve this
Status: D0 watch percentage among dormant users is "very bad sign" and "biggest red flag"

Retention deep-dive:
- D1 retention is "comparatively much weaker" than D3
- Biggest factor for renewal: recency to renewal date (NOT early habit formation)
- "Habit moment" = 8 active watch days in first month
- M1+ watch % = users who watched in last 28 days (most active cohort)

Pan-India Expansion

Timeline: Within next 45 days
Languages launching: Punjabi, Bengali, Assami, Odia, Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam
Still under discussion: Hindi content strategy

Insight: Regional content has less reach but high revenue per acquired user

Cultural wrapper concept:
- Makes platform part of user's consideration set
- BUT only works if core filmmaking quality is good
- Risk: Being seen as "language dialect first" vs "culture first" company

Financial Context

Fundraising:
- Series C campaign launching soon
- Venture debt being raised for 6-month cash flow bridge
- Investor expectation: profitability growth + retention + MoM revenue growth

Operating reality: Every decision must solve for next 9 months of cash flow

Team Structure Philosophy

Three hypotheses:
1. Every team member is a full-stack builder
2. One person at senior level owns one metric
3. Small teams tackle problem statements with clear accountability

Key Decisions on SR (Subscription Rate)

Why SR needs separate ownership:
- It's primarily a revenue metric and an output
- Driven by lower TCR + better watch retention
- Quality of acquisition impacts subscription quality
- But SR must be maximized as separate accountability

Model agreed: Shubham has complete ownership of acquisition piece, others assist/collaborate

Reacquisition vs Reactivation (Critical Distinction)

Reactivation:
- Target: trial takers who didn't subscribe or churned subscribers
- Focus: re-engage existing relationship
- Owner: Alia (proposed)

Reacquisition:
- Target: non-trial-takers (never activated)
- Focus: bring back cold users
- Owner: Jagrati (proposed)

Principle: These are fundamentally different and require different people/strategies

Renewal Rate Strategy

Working assumption: Increasing watching might improve renewal retention
Proposed approach: Decouple SR/TCR focus from watch-driven retention

Two separate optimizations:
1. Increase users who pay
2. Increase users who watch

Critical point: Coach Retention is "fundamentally the first and most important thing to solve" to avoid repeating negative situations
