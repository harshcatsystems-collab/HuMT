The Pipeline: Synopsis → Shooting Draft

  1. Format Selection: Feature film, series, or "I don't know yet"
  2. Structure: Linear, nonlinear, etc.
  3. World-Building: 4 key questions — World, Texture, Wounds, Allure
  4. Synopsis: ~150 words
  5. Short Story: 500–1,000 words
  6. Character Bible: 6-step process
  7. Screenplay: Action + dialogue
  8. Final Shooting Draft: All scenes, dialogues, shot breakdowns

Every step has a supporting agent. Key insight from Shashank: don't make the writer go through all 13 steps manually — AI generates, humans evaluate and iterate.

Architecture Decisions

  • DECISION Pre-production between ALL agents — every agent onboarded from day one with same context. Automatic alignment, no drift.
  • DECISION Architect for video NOW — prompt-to-video is a "when problem, not if problem." Build comprehensively now, avoid re-engineering later.
  • DECISION Micro-specific agents >> macro agents — highly specific agents outperform general-purpose ones.
  • DECISION Mirror professional film process — automate thinking like costume designers, lighting experts, etc.
