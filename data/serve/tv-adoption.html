03 The Bimodal Activation Pattern

Users either try once and leave, or become power users. There's nothing in between.

If 1,500 new users try TV weekly but WAU only grows marginally, ~90% of first-time TV users churn within a week. Yet the ones who stay achieve 66-70% W4 retention.

The empty middle: This bimodal pattern (try-once vs power-user) means the problem is activation quality, not product quality. The TV experience is excellent — but the first session fails to hook most users. Fix the first session, and you unlock the middle.

Five Barriers to TV Activation

AWARENESS

Users don't know STAGE has a TV app

No mention in mobile onboarding. No "Watch on TV" prompt. No push notifications. It's invisible.

FRICTION

TV login requires typing on a TV keyboard

Find app → download → login on TV keyboard → browse content. Each step loses 50-70% of users.

DEVICE ACCESS

Not every household has a connected TV

India shipped 15M+ smart TVs in 2025 — the base is growing. But casting could bridge the gap now.

MENTAL MODEL

"STAGE is a phone app"

Users discovered STAGE on mobile. The conceptual leap to TV doesn't happen naturally — it must be pushed.

CONTENT FORMAT

5-min microdramas don't feel "TV-worthy"

TV needs long-form as the hook. If a user's first experience is a short clip, they won't think to go to TV.

06 The Bhojpuri TV Gap

STAGE's largest dialect by subscribers. Nearly absent on TV. This is the biggest untapped opportunity.

Bhojpuri — Subscriber Share

~40%

Bhojpuri — TV WAU Share

9%

If Bhojpuri TV adoption were proportional to its subscriber base, TV WAU would be ~1,600 for Bhojpuri alone — vs the current 347. That's a potential +1,250 high-value TV users.

Why Bhojpuri Is Missing

Content depth: Only 23 Bhojpuri microdramas vs 44 Haryanvi. Long-format series are thinner. TV viewing demands enough content to fill multiple sessions.

Smart TV penetration: Bihar, Jharkhand, and eastern UP likely have lower smart TV adoption than Haryana. This is a structural ceiling — unless you solve it with casting.

Format mismatch: If Bhojpuri content skews short-form, users don't associate it with TV viewing. "Jaan Legi Sonam" (Bhojpuri long series) is new — needs time.

Recommendation: Treat Bhojpuri TV as a separate growth project with its own WAU target, content roadmap, and activation experiments. Don't let it ride on the Haryanvi-centric status quo.

10 Why Hasn't This Happened Already?

An honest assessment of likely barriers — and why the moment is now

Resource allocation. STAGE is a startup. Engineering goes to mobile because that's 98% of users. But TV isn't a feature — it's a multiplier. Every TV user is worth 2-3 mobile users.

Measurement gap. TV impact wasn't quantified until this dashboard. You can't invest in what you can't measure. Now you can.

The chicken-and-egg. TV needs content to attract users, but content investment follows user numbers. TV has 4K users → minimal investment → stays at 4K. This data breaks the cycle.

Device access uncertainty. "Do our users have smart TVs?" India shipped 15M+ smart TVs in 2025. Casting eliminates the smart TV requirement entirely.

The question isn't "should we invest in TV?"
It's "how fast can we move?"
