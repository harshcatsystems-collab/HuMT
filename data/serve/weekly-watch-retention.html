WoW retention dropped from 50.3% → 40.8% in 6 weeks, with a partial recovery to 44.5%. Simultaneously, the watcher base contracted 38%. Both trends compound: fewer watchers AND lower return rates.

The Jan board deck showed STAGE at #3 in India for M1 retention (53%). The live dashboard shows weekly retention declining. These aren't contradictory — M1 measures monthly subscriber retention, this measures weekly watching habits. But the weekly habit is what sustains the monthly number. If weekly watching erodes, M1 will follow within 1-2 quarters.

Strategic Recommendations

P0 This Week

  • Content calendar audit Map weekly release dates against the Jan 26 inflection. If Mahapunarjanam S1 ended without a replacement anchor show, that's likely the trigger for the retention drop. Fill the gap immediately.
  • Resurrect 1 intervention acceleration Move CLM triggers for 1-week inactive users to day 1 of inactivity. The current cadence is too slow — every day of delay costs ~1pp recovery probability.
  • D_31to60 emergency deep-dive This segment collapsed 15pp. Pull user-level data: last content watched, session before churn, dialect breakdown. Find the pattern driving the exodus.

P1 This Sprint (2 Weeks)

  • Cross-format exposure in M0 Board deck proved 8pp M0 lift for users consuming both micro-drama and long-format. Every new subscriber should see both formats within their first session. This is a product/algo change — high ROI, low cost.
  • Bhojpuri content acceleration 67.5K M0 subs (largest dialect) but 2.51% watch conversion vs 3.5% for Haryanvi/Rajasthani. Only 23 microdramas vs 44 Haryanvi. Close the content gap.
  • Renewal user TV push 60% of watchers are renewals. TV users = 2.4x D30 retention. Targeted "Watch on TV" campaign for renewal users. Protect the backbone with the highest-ROI channel.

P2 This Month

  • Algorithmic freshness overhaul Repeat 2 decline suggests stale recommendations. Inject diversity — ensure repeat visitors see new/unseen content in top slots.
  • Weekly content velocity target Set minimum new episodes/week across dialects. Track alongside retention. Content drought = retention drought.
  • "Home show" matching at D_15 The week 3-4 cliff is a content discovery failure. Test: assign a prominent recommended series on return visits with progress tracking and "next episode" nudges.
  • Watcher base recovery target Set explicit goal: 100K watchers by end of March. Track weekly inflow (new + resurrected) vs outflow (lapsed). Make it a north star.

Bottom Line

STAGE built something real. #3 M1 retention in India. Proven CLM framework. Strong content-market fit. The structural improvements from 2025 are intact.

But the live data shows the gains are leaking. Mid-funnel (weeks 3-8) is collapsing. Core users are degrading. The watcher base is shrinking. This isn't a crisis yet — renewal users are holding — but without action on content velocity, mid-funnel engagement, and resurrection timing, the 50% → 41% slide will continue.

At 87K watchers, there's less margin for error than at 141K. The next 4 weeks will determine whether Feb was a seasonal dip or the start of a structural decline.
