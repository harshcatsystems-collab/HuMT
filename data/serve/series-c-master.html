Series C Narrative — Deck Structure (Post-Vivek Feedback)

Recommended Flow

  1. Cover — Hook/excitement
  2. Problem + Market (TAM) — Merged. 1.5B people, 22 languages, 19K dialects, <0.5% served. Get people excited about the size of the problem.
  3. Why is this market underserved? — Structural cost problem (not competitor bashing). "Cost of production too high → must amortize across large user base → only Hindi gets served."
  4. What we've built (high level) — Summarize + list components: consumer experience, creation pipeline, cultural adaptation, one IP → many cultures, AI economics
  5. Engagement story (was "Retention") — The 12-month journey: Dec raised → Jan stopped perf marketing → focused retention → 4 months figuring out → May started fixing → results. Show timeline + retention curve. Competitive data if available.
  6. Market playbook — Every new market scales faster ("money slide" per Vivek)
  7. Transforming STAGE into an AI native business — The pivot story. Transition slide.
  8. AI: Consumer experience — Character bots, personalization, discovery
  9. AI: End-to-end creation pipeline — Agentic movie production, 11-question story process
  10. AI: Cultural adaptation — 155 markers, 16 categories, 1 scene → 6 cultures
  11. AI: One IP → many cultures — Productization of culture
  12. What AI does to economics — Cost reduction, speed, scale
  13. How we win — Three pillars: (1) Distribution/first-mover, (2) Brand/customer trust, (3) Cultural context & storytelling. Acknowledge AI is replicable → speed + culture are moats.
  14. Growth plan — 4 → 14 markets
  15. Financial projections
  16. Global vision — Icing on the cake
  17. The ask
  18. Appendix — AI tools detail, product screenshots, supporting data
