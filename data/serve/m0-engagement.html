The Zero-Activity Problem: Root Cause Analysis

My position: This is primarily an acquisition quality problem, not an engagement problem. Most of these 110K "subscribers" never intended to use STAGE.

Why I believe this:

  1. The math doesn't work for "bad onboarding." If users consciously chose STAGE, even terrible onboarding would yield >35% app-open. Zero activity on 65% means most never opened the app at all.
  2. Carrier billing / bundled acquisition. In India's regional OTT space, telco bundles are a primary acquisition channel. Users get "subscribed" via data pack upsells without active consent. They're counted as M0 subs but were never prospects.
  3. Uninstall rate is only 32.5%. If 65% are inactive but only 32.5% uninstall, many haven't even installed the app to uninstall it. They exist only as billing records.

What to do about it:

Stop measuring activation on the full base. Segment by acquisition channel. Measure activation rate per source. You'll likely find that organic/referral channels have 40-60% activation, while carrier billing has <10%. Kill or renegotiate low-activation channels. Report M0 metrics on "intentional subscribers" separately.

Experiment: Tag every new sub with acquisition source. In 2 weeks, you'll have the data to make surgical cuts to acquisition spend.

Bhojpuri has 30% more subscribers than Rajasthani but 29% lower watch conversion. On the largest dialect base, this 1pp gap = ~675 missing daily watchers.

Four hypotheses (ranked by likelihood):

1. Content-format mismatch. Bhojpuri entertainment consumption on YouTube is dominated by music videos, comedy clips, and short-form. STAGE's long-form movies/shows may not match the existing consumption habit. Haryanvi and Rajasthani audiences may have a stronger appetite for narrative content.

2. Acquisition channel skew. If Bhojpuri over-indexes on carrier billing (larger addressable market in UP/Bihar = more telco partnerships), the denominator is more inflated than other dialects.

3. Content depth and quality. How many Bhojpuri titles vs Haryanvi/Rajasthani? What's the production value? If the catalog is thinner or lower-quality, conversion will suffer.

4. Free alternative dominance. Bhojpuri has the most vibrant free content ecosystem on YouTube (billions of views). The "why pay when YouTube is free" barrier is highest for Bhojpuri.

Recommended action: Pull three data points this week: (1) Bhojpuri catalog size vs other dialects, (2) acquisition channel mix per dialect, (3) Bhojpuri content completion rates vs others. The answer is in the intersection.

The Conversion Ceiling: Why 3% Won't Break

Watch conversion has oscillated between 2.86% and 3.25% for 10+ days with no breakout. This is a structural ceiling, not a variance problem.

Why the ceiling exists:

The engaged get more engaged; the disengaged stay disengaged. Watch frequency improves (2.17 days) because existing watchers find more to watch. But no mechanism converts non-watchers into watchers. These are two separate populations with different needs.

The zero-activity pool is unreachable in-app. You can't improve in-app conversion for users who never open the app. Push notifications hit a wall when 65% aren't installed.

The 28.5K "opened once" users are the breakout opportunity. These users showed intent (opened app) but didn't watch. Current onboarding likely shows a content grid — expecting users to browse and choose. Regional dialect audiences may need a more directed first experience.
