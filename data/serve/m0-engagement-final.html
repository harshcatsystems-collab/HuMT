Key Finding: The 58% Non-Watcher Problem

58% of M0 subscribers (110K users) have ZERO watch activity. This is the single biggest engagement lever. Even a 5-10% shift from non-watchers to watchers would add 5.5K-11K to the engaged base.

🔬 Diagnosis — What's Broken & Why

Three-Layer Engagement Problem

❌ Layer 1: First Watch Barrier (58% never watch)

Problem: Over half of M0 subscribers never consume a single piece of content during their 30-day trial.

Why it matters: You can't build habit if users never start. This is an activation/onboarding problem, not a content problem.

Contributing factors:

  • Subscription ≠ Intent to watch (impulse ₹1 trials converting without genuine interest)
  • App install gap: 32.5% uninstall rate suggests many subscribe but never set up properly
  • Onboarding friction: No compelling "first watch" trigger post-subscription
  • Zero-activity bucket (110K users) includes both "not interested" AND "didn't know how to start"

⚠️ Layer 2: Watcher→Habit Cliff (42% → 4.7% = 89% drop-off)

Problem: 42% of M0 subs watch at least once, but only 4.7% develop habit (8+ days). That's an 89% drop-off from first watch to sustained engagement.

Why it matters: We're getting users to try the content, but failing to convert trial into routine.

Contributing factors:

  • Content discovery problem: Users watch once, don't find next compelling piece
  • Lack of viewing ritual/trigger: No daily/weekly content drop pattern users can anchor to
  • Insufficient personalization: Generic homepage doesn't guide second/third watch
  • Trial activity bucket correlation: Low trial engagement (0-1 active days during trial) predicts low M0 engagement

📉 Layer 3: Daily Activation Ceiling (3.1% daily conversion stuck)

Problem: Daily watch conversion oscillates in a tight 2.86%-3.25% band with no upward trend despite improving avg watch days per watcher.

Why it matters: Engaged users are watching MORE (2.17 days/watcher, up from 1.93), but we're not expanding the engaged base.

Contributing factors:

  • The 110K zero-activity base acts as dead weight on daily conversion metrics
  • Notification/reminder strategy may not be reaching inactive users
  • Daily content release cadence may not align with user availability

Critical Insight: Trial Engagement is M0's Leading Indicator

The 0_0 bucket (58% of base, 0.58% Watcher%) is WHERE THE PROBLEM LIVES.

  • Users who don't engage during trial (zero active days) almost never engage in M0
  • Every additional trial active day roughly DOUBLES M0 Watcher%: 0.58% → 3.47% → 8.34% → 16.7% → 30.2%
  • Root cause: Trial activation failure cascades into M0 engagement failure
  • Strategic implication: Fixing M0 engagement starts with fixing TRIAL engagement

🎯 Action Plan — How to Fix

Immediate Actions (Week 1-2)

1. Attack the 110K Zero-Activity Base

Goal: Move 10% of zero-activity users (11K) to first watch within 14 days

Tactics:

  • Day 1-3 activation flow: SMS + push campaign highlighting top 3 trending titles in user's dialect
  • Personalized content preview: Email with 30-sec clips of 5 recommended titles (no login required)
  • Onboarding nudge: "Your ₹1 trial unlocked 800 hours of [Dialect] content. Start with these 3 blockbusters."
  • Friction audit: Test login flow, app download, first playback on 10 devices. Identify and fix blockers.

Success metric: Reduce 0_0 bucket from 58% to 50% of M0 base

2. Fix Bhojpuri Engagement Gap

Goal: Lift Bhojpuri daily conversion from 2.51% to 3.0% (match platform avg)

Tactics:

  • Content audit: Survey 100 Bhojpuri M0 subs (engaged vs non-engaged) on content preferences
  • Localization check: Validate Bhojpuri content authenticity (dialect variants, regional themes)
  • Release cadence test: Try daily vs weekly drops, track engagement delta
  • Quality uplift: Compare Bhojpuri vs Haryanvi content production values, close gaps

Success metric: +330 daily Bhojpuri watchers within 30 days

Short-Term Actions (Week 3-6)

3. Build Watcher → Habit Bridge

Goal: Improve Watcher→Habit conversion from 11% to 20%

Tactics:

  • "Day 2" content recommendation: After first watch, immediately surface 3 related titles
  • Viewing streak gamification: "You've watched 2 days in a row! Keep it going for 7 days to unlock..."
  • Scheduled notifications: "Your favorite actor's new movie drops tomorrow at 8 PM"
  • Personalized homepage: HP Personalisation (Encanto algorithm) prioritization for M0 users

Success metric: Habit% increases from 4.7% to 7.5%

4. Trial Engagement Uplift (Root Cause Fix)

Goal: Reduce 0_0 trial activity bucket from 58% to 40%

Tactics:

  • Trial onboarding overhaul: Mandatory "Watch 1 video to activate subscription" step
  • Immediate gratification: Auto-play top trending title on first app open post-subscription
  • Trial engagement nudges: "3 days left in your trial. Still haven't watched? Here's what's hot."
  • A/B test trial lengths: 3-day vs 7-day trials, track M0 engagement delta

Success metric: 70% of new subs have ≥1 watch day during trial (up from 42%)

Long-Term Initiatives (6-12 weeks)

5. Platform-Specific Optimization

  • App users: Lean into push notifications, home screen widgets, offline download prompts
  • Web users: Email-based engagement (daily digest, new releases), browser notifications
  • Cross-platform sync: "Continue watching" across devices to reduce friction

6. Cohort-Based Personalization

  • Week 1 users: Focus on activation (first watch trigger)
  • Week 2-3 users: Focus on habit formation (streak building)
  • Week 4 users: Focus on retention (prepare for M1 with "subscriber exclusive" content tease)

7. App Install Incentive

  • Hypothesis: App users have 2-3x higher engagement than web-only
  • Tactic: "Install the app to unlock 10 exclusive titles" + easier offline access
  • Target: Reduce uninstall rate from 32.5% to 25%, increase app-install-at-subscription from (unknown baseline) to 70%

🎓 Key Learnings

What This Analysis Reveals

  1. M0 engagement is a THREE-LAYER problem: Activation (58% never watch) → Habit Formation (89% drop from first watch to 8+ days) → Daily Activation (stuck at 3.1%)
  2. Trial activity predicts M0 activity: Zero trial engagement → 0.58% M0 Watcher%. High trial engagement (8+ days) → 30.2% M0 Watcher%. Fix trial, fix M0.
  3. Content quality is improving: Avg watch days per watcher climbing steadily (1.93 → 2.17). The problem isn't content value for engaged users.
  4. Dialect matters: Bhojpuri lags 28% behind Har/Raj despite being 41% of base. Fixing this unlocks 13% more daily watchers.
  5. The 110K zero-activity base is the leverage point: Moving even 10% of them to first watch would add 11K to engaged base — 220% increase in daily watchers.
