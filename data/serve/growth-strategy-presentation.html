Hypothesis A: Acquisition Degraded

Early users were organic, dialect-enthusiastic. Current users come from scaled performance marketing. The 34% zero-watchers didn't exist in early cohorts. You're buying volume, not intent.

Hypothesis B: AHA Definition Stale

If AHA requires multi-day engagement, and D1 return is structurally 15%, recent cohorts mathematically can't achieve AHA at old rates — even if equally engaged on D0.

Position: It's mostly A. The M0 zero-activity rate (65%), growing never-watched HVU pool (+30% in 4 months), and Futwork zero-incremental data all point to acquisition quality degradation. STAGE is buying volume, not watchers.

12 / WHAT TO STOP

Stop Doing These

✕

Futwork OB Calling

Zero incremental conversions for 4+ days. 5-7K calls/day with no marginal lift. Pause immediately, run A/B test, redirect budget.

✕

Campaigns with >50% Zero-Watch Rate

You're paying to create dormants. Score every campaign by 7-day engagement. Kill the ghosts.

✕

Generic Push Notifications

"Come back and watch!" doesn't work. Only personalized, content-specific pushes. "Episode 3 of Husband on Sale is waiting."

✕

Optimizing for Trial Volume

The north star should be D1 returners, not trial starts. 13K trials/day means nothing when 79% are wasted.

✕

LVU Dormant Targeting

375K never-watched LVUs at 0.26% conversion. These are carrier billing ghosts. Write them off. Focus calling on 214K HVU+MVU previously-watched.
