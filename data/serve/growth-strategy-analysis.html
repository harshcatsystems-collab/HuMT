STAGE Growth Strategy: The Funnel Is a Sieve

For HMT | Feb 23, 2026 | Confidential

Executive Summary

STAGE acquires 10-13K trials daily. 79% of them are wasted. The growth funnel isn't leaking — it's structurally broken at the D0→D1 transition. This document maps every critical growth metric, identifies root causes, and proposes the 3-5 highest-leverage interventions.

The single most important number: 65% watch on D0. 15% watch on D1. That 50pp cliff determines everything downstream — AHA rates, habit formation, mandate success, and the 1.1M dormant pool. Fix D1 return and every downstream metric moves.

Even the best-case scenario (60+ min D0 watchers) loses 55-65% by D1. This isn't an engagement problem. This is a RECALL problem. Users forget STAGE exists.

Root Cause Analysis

Why don't engaged D0 users come back on D1?

1. No unfinished business. Microdrama (the dominant D0 content) is designed to be consumed in one sitting. 70-88% of trial users watch microdrama on D0. They finish. There's no cliffhanger pulling them back. Compare to Netflix: you're always mid-episode, mid-season.

2. No recall mechanism. Push notification permission rates for new installs in India average 40-60%. If STAGE is at industry average, half of D0 users simply never get reminded. For web users (25% of trials), there's zero push capability.

3. No habit loop. The app has no daily reason to open — no feed, no social element, no gamification, no streak, no "new episode dropped" notification tied to something they watched.

4. Content discovery is front-loaded. Users see the best content on D0 (algorithm surfaces hits). D1 feels like "I already saw the good stuff." The library needs to reveal depth over time, not breadth on day one.

Competitive Context

STAGE's D1 retention is 20% (board deck metric, slightly different from the 15% trial-specific data). Benchmarks:

  • Netflix: 42%
  • JioHotstar: 35%
  • ZEE5/SonyLIV: 28%
  • STAGE: 20% (#9 of 12)

STAGE is near the bottom of D1. Yet at M1, STAGE is #3 (53%). This paradox reveals: users who survive D1 are incredibly loyal, but the D1 gate is too narrow.

  • Carrier billing flows where subscription is bundled/accidental
  • Performance marketing optimized for trial starts, not engagement
  • Misleading creatives that attract non-target-audience clicks

The 45% One-Day Watchers

These users did find something to watch. They consumed content. And they never came back. This is the D0→D1 cliff population. They're not low-quality — they're unrecovered.

Two Possible Explanations

Hypothesis A: Acquisition quality degraded 3x. Early STAGE users were organic, high-intent, dialect-enthusiastic. Current users come from scaled performance marketing. The 34% zero-watchers didn't exist in early cohorts.

Hypothesis B: AHA definition hasn't scaled with product. If AHA requires multi-day engagement, and D1 return is structurally broken, recent cohorts mathematically can't achieve AHA at old rates — even if they're equally engaged on D0.

My position: It's both, but mostly A. The M0 zero-activity rate (65%) and the growing never-watched dormant HVU pool (+30% in 4 months) both point to acquisition quality. STAGE is buying volume, not intent.

The 1.1M dormants are the graveyard of growth failures. Every month, ~300K+ new trials enter the funnel. ~237K (79%) fall through. They become the dormants that the CLM team then tries to resurrect at 0.28% daily conversion. It's a losing game.

Fix growth activation and retention fixes itself. The board deck proves this: M1 retention is 53% (#3 in India). Users who survive activation are loyal. The funnel's neck is narrow, not leaky at the bottom.

9. Strategy Forward: The 5 Highest-Leverage Bets

BET 1: Solve D1 Return (Expected Impact: +15-20pp D1 retention)

The problem: Users forget STAGE exists. No unfinished business, no recall mechanism.

Build:

  • Cliffhanger architecture. Every microdrama series should end episodes mid-tension. "Next episode" auto-queue with 24-hour unlock for free content. Create artificial incompleteness.
  • Aggressive D0 push notification opt-in. Full-screen prompt during first watch session (not at install). "Get notified when Episode 2 drops." Benchmark: 70%+ opt-in during content consumption.
  • D1 "your show continued" notification. Personalized, tied to exactly what they watched. Not generic "come back" — specific "Episode 3 of Husband on Sale is waiting."
  • Web→App bridge. For the 25% on web: hard push to install app during D0 session. "Install to get notified when new episodes drop." Every web user without the app is a guaranteed D1 loss.

Why this is #1: A 15pp improvement in D1 (from 15% to 30%) doubles the pool of users who enter the habit formation window. At 13K trials/day, that's 1,950 additional D1 users daily — 58K/month more users entering the retention funnel.

BET 2: Kill Zero-Watchers at Source (Expected Impact: -15pp zero-watch rate)

The problem: 34% of trials never watch a single minute. 65% of M0 subs have zero activity.

Build:

  • Auto-play on trial activation. The moment payment is confirmed, content starts playing. No home screen, no browse, no choice paralysis. Microdrama Episode 1 begins. Based on dialect preference, serve the highest-completion show (Husband on Sale for Haryanvi, Jholachhap for Bhojpuri).
  • Acquisition quality scoring. Tag every acquisition channel/campaign by 7-day engagement rate. Kill campaigns with >50% zero-watch rate. Redirect spend to channels producing watchers, not installers.
  • Carrier billing audit. If a meaningful portion of zero-watchers come from carrier billing bundles, renegotiate or eliminate those channels. They're polluting every metric.

Why this is #2: Moving 15pp from zero-watch (34% → 19%) adds ~2K daily watchers to the D0 funnel. If those users watch at average rates, ~300 will convert to D1, ~60 will form habits. And you stop polluting the dormant pool.

BET 3: Microdrama as Gateway (Expected Impact: +5pp cross-format consumption)

The problem: Microdrama activates D0 beautifully (70-88% of trial users watch it) but doesn't create D1 return.

Build:

  • Microdrama→Long-format bridge. Data shows D1 non-microdrama watch (18%) > D1 same-microdrama (13%). Microdramas are already a gateway. Make it intentional: after completing a microdrama, recommend the related long-format show. "Liked Husband on Sale? Mewat has 26 episodes."
  • Continuous discovery loop. After one microdrama ends, auto-queue the next. Don't let the session end. The 120+ min D0 watchers (13.4% AHA) got there by chaining content. Make chaining the default path.
  • Fix the CTR crash. Microdrama CTR dropped from 1.28% to 0.62% after Feb 19. Something broke in discovery/recommendation. Find it and fix it — this directly impacts D0 activation.

Why this is #3: Cross-format users have 48% M0 retention vs 40% single-format. The 8pp lift at scale (applied to 10K+ daily users) compounds through the entire retention curve.

BET 4: TV Push (Expected Impact: 2x TV penetration to 4-5%)

The problem: TV users retain at 2.4x D30 and watch 5x more. But TV is only 2% of the base. 1.2% of trial users ever activate on TV.

Build:

  • TV activation prompt at AHA moment. When a user hits AHA (demonstrated engagement), surface "Watch on your TV — it's 5x better." Don't push TV to everyone; push it to users who've proven they love the content.
  • Simplified TV setup. QR code scan from phone → instant TV app activation. Zero friction. One-tap casting from phone to TV.
  • TV-first content premieres. Premiere key titles on TV first (24-hour exclusive) to drive TV adoption among engaged users.

Why this is #4: Doubling TV from 2% to 4% of base at 2.4x retention lift adds meaningful retention improvement system-wide. But it's a smaller absolute pool than D1 or zero-watcher fixes.

BET 5: Bhojpuri Content Parity (Expected Impact: +1pp overall M0 watch conversion)

The problem: Bhojpuri is the largest subscriber base but lowest engagement. 23 microdramas vs 44 Haryanvi.

Build:

  • Double Bhojpuri microdrama library in 90 days (23 → 46 titles). Focus on proven formats: relationship drama (Husband on Sale Bho already works), crime/thriller.
  • Bhojpuri long-format weekly series. Jaan Legi Sonam exists but it's alone. Need 2-3 concurrent weekly shows to create a reason to return.

Why this is #5: Bhojpuri's 67.5K M0 base at 2.51% watch conversion vs 3.5% Har/Raj means closing the gap adds ~660 daily watchers. Meaningful but requires content investment timeline.

11. The 90-Day Growth Math

Current state (daily):

  • 13K trials → 8,450 watch on D0 (65%) → 1,950 return D1 (15%) → 507 reach AHA (3.9%) → 3,510 convert to paid (27%)
  • Net active watchers after 30 days: ~87K (total base)

After interventions (90-day target):

  • 13K trials (same volume) → 10,530 watch on D0 (81%, from killing zero-watchers) → 3,159 return D1 (30%, from recall mechanisms) → 948 reach AHA (30% of D1 returners) → 4,290 convert to paid (33%)
  • Key improvement: D1 returners 1.6x (1,950 → 3,159/day)
  • That's 36K additional D1 users per month entering the retention funnel
  • At 53% M1 retention: 19K additional M1 active users/month
  • At 22% M3 retention: 7.9K additional M3 active users/month
  • In 90 days: +24K active base from growth funnel fix alone

The dormant math also improves: With fewer zero-watchers and more D1 returners, the monthly inflow to the dormant pool drops by ~36K. Over 90 days, that's 108K fewer dormants created. The 1.1M dormant problem starts shrinking instead of growing.

Summary: The One Slide

STAGE's growth funnel wastes 79% of trials. The D0→D1 cliff (50pp drop) is the single largest value destroyer. Even engaged D0 users don't return — this is a recall problem, not a content problem.

Fix five things in order:

1. D1 return (cliffhangers, push notifications, "your show continued")

2. Zero-watchers (auto-play, acquisition quality scoring, carrier billing audit)

3. Microdrama gateway (cross-format bridge, continuous discovery, fix CTR crash)

4. TV push (to proven engaged users only)

5. Bhojpuri parity (double microdrama library)

Stop: Futwork OB calling, LVU dormant targeting, generic pushes, optimizing for trial volume.

North star change: Stop measuring trial starts. Start measuring D1 returners.
