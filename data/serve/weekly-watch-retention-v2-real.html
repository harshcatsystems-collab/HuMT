M1 Retention Deep Dive: What The Time Series Reveals

  • Two critical drops in mid-January — M1 retention fell to 79.1% (Jan 12) and 73.6% (Jan 19), losing 20-26% of watchers week-over-week. These were the only two "critical" weeks for M1 cohort.
  • Recovery pattern emerging — After Jan 19 low point, M1 retention gradually improved: 92.2% → 85.1% → 89.0% → 101.7%. The trend is upward despite absolute numbers declining.
  • M1 users stick around despite drops — Even in worst weeks, M1 retained 73-79% of prior week's watchers. Compare to M0's worst weeks with 63-65% retention—M1 shows 10-15% stronger retention floor.
  • Late Feb stabilization (+1.7% growth) — Week of Feb 16 shows first positive WoW growth, suggesting M1 cohort has found a stable engagement baseline around 8,700 daily watchers.
  • Average 88.7% weekly retention — Across 7 weeks of data, M1 users retained at nearly 9 in 10 week-over-week. This is 23 percentage points higher than M0's 65.8% average.
  • External shock hypothesis — The synchronized Jan 12-19 collapse across both M0 and M1 suggests content/competitive/seasonal factors, not just onboarding issues. M1's faster recovery (4 weeks vs M0's ongoing decline) shows resilience.
  • Recommendation — M1 cohort is performing well structurally. Focus retention efforts on M0→M1 conversion (the 30-day cliff), not on M1 retention itself. M1 users who survived 30 days have strong habits.

Strategic Insights: M0 vs M1 Retention Behavior

  • M0 users are driving the decline — M0 cohort declined 81.8% vs M1's 58.4%, indicating new subscribers are struggling to establish watch habits
  • M1 users show resilience — Despite overall decline, M1 stabilized in late Feb (+1.7% WoW), suggesting established users maintain engagement once they pass the 30-day mark
  • Critical retention window: First 30 days — M0's steeper decline (-36.3% single week) vs M1's worst week (-26.4%) shows new subscriber onboarding is the vulnerability point
  • M1 users watch 3x more — 3.09x ratio suggests that users who survive the M0 period become highly engaged, making M0→M1 conversion the key metric
  • Late Jan collapse (Jan 12-26) — Both cohorts saw accelerated decline mid-January, suggesting external factors (content drought, festival season ending, competition) affected all users
  • Feb 2 was M0's breaking point — -36.3% WoW is catastrophic; coincides with deepest M0 engagement loss. Investigate what changed in early Feb content/UX
  • Recommendation — Focus retention interventions on M0 cohort (first 30 days): personalized onboarding, content recommendations, habit-building nudges. M1 retention is healthier and stabilizing
