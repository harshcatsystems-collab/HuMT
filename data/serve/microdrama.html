The one-line story: Microdramas are a trial activation engine (75-90% D0), not a retention vehicle. Users who consume both microdramas AND long-format retain at 48% M0 vs 40% single-format. Microdramas are the gateway. Long-format is the product.

Three urgent signals:

🔴 CTR crashed 52% after Feb 19 (1.28% → 0.62%) — discovery is broken

🟡 Duration sweet spot = 1-1.3hrs — every show over 1.5hrs dies on completion

🟡 Haryanvi outperforms Bhojpuri on every metric — dialect quality gap is widening

Key insight: Single-format users retain identically (~40% M0) regardless of which format. The 8pp lift comes entirely from cross-format exposure. Microdramas alone ≠ better retention. Microdramas + long-format = better retention.

Strategic frame: Stop measuring microdrama success by microdrama metrics. The right KPI is cross-format conversion rate — % of microdrama users who watch long-format within 7 days.

9 in 10 trial users watch on day 0. Fewer than 2 in 10 come back on day 1. This isn't unique to microdrama — STAGE overall is #9 in D1 retention (20%) among OTT peers.

The gateway needs a door on both sides. Currently, users get pulled in on D0 but nothing structured pulls them back on D1. The D1 push notification with a personalized microdrama + long-format pairing is the single highest-leverage CLM intervention.

Note: Feb 22 D1 = 1.5% — likely incomplete data (only 1 day elapsed). Don't alarm on this number yet.

10

Strategic Recommendations

P0 This Week

1. Investigate CTR Crash (Feb 19)
Pull product changelog, check UI/algo/tracking changes. 52% CTR decline sustained 4+ days = potential trial activation collapse. If product change caused it, revert or A/B test immediately.
2. Implement Cross-Format Bridge
After every microdrama completion, surface a long-format recommendation. The 48% vs 40% M0 gap is proven. Force the exposure algorithmically — end cards, "watch next" rails, first-session sequencing.
3. Hard Duration Cap on Commissions
Maximum 4,800 seconds (80 minutes). No exceptions. Every minute over 80 costs completion rate. If the story needs more time, make it a series.

P1 This Month

4. D1 Re-Engagement Sequence
Push notification at D1 with personalized microdrama + long-format pairing. The D0→D1 cliff (90% → 15%) is the single biggest drop in the funnel. Automated, personalized D1 push is highest-leverage CLM intervention.
5. Kill Underperformers
Any show below 40% Hook→Complete: deprioritize in recommendation, don't renew. Reallocate budget to proven archetypes (relationship drama, 1hr Haryanvi).
6. Bhojpuri Quality Audit
Before adding Bhojpuri microdramas, audit existing titles for production quality, pacing, hook structure. Why do Bhojpuri originals underperform Haryanvi localizations? Fix quality before expanding quantity.

P2 This Quarter

7. Microdrama-to-Series Pipeline
Top microdrama concepts (husband-on-sale, crorepati-biwi) should be expanded into long-format series. Microdrama proves demand; series captures retention. This is the natural extension of the Gateway Hypothesis.
8. New KPI: Cross-Format Conversion Rate
Track % of microdrama-only users who watch long-format within 7 days. This is the true measure of microdrama's strategic value — not microdrama retention or microdrama watch time.
9. Session Depth Optimization
2.46 sessions/user, 25.9 episodes/user suggests binge patterns exist. Optimize autoplay/next-up to extend 30 min avg sessions toward 45 min. More time in-app = more cross-format exposure.
—

The Bottom Line

Microdramas = customer acquisition cost reduction.
Long-format = lifetime value.

Every microdrama dollar should be evaluated by its contribution to cross-format conversion and long-format retention. The 48% vs 40% M0 number is the north star. The CTR crash is the immediate fire. The duration discipline is the commissioning rule. The cross-format bridge is the product priority.
