The one-line story: Microdramas hook hard on D0 (84% of MD-first users watch), but users who stay engaged migrate to long-format. Users who consume both microdramas AND long-format retain at 48% M0 vs 40% single-format. Microdramas are the gateway. Long-format is the product.

Three urgent signals:

🔴 CTR crashed 52% after Feb 19 (1.28% → 0.62%) — homepage layout shift broke discovery

🟡 Duration sweet spot = 1-1.3hrs — every show over 1.5hrs dies on completion

🟢 Content migration pattern is real — MD-first users shift to long-form naturally (need to accelerate)

02

The Gateway Hypothesis

Microdramas drive trial activation but long-format drives retention. The data is definitive.

Cross-Format Retention (Board Deck Data)

SegmentM0M1M2
Microdrama only40.2%19.9%15.4%
Long-format only39.6%19.6%14.9%
Both formats48.1%26.3%20.2%

Key insight: Single-format users retain identically (~40% M0) regardless of which format. The 8pp lift comes entirely from cross-format exposure. Microdramas alone ≠ better retention. Microdramas + long-format = better retention.

Strategic frame: Stop measuring microdrama success by microdrama metrics. The right KPI is cross-format conversion rate — % of microdrama users who watch long-format within 7 days.

CRITICAL FINDING: Microdrama-first users migrate to long-format content during trial. By D1, more users are watching long-format (12.3%) than microdrama (7.6%). By D6, long-format viewership is 2.3x microdrama.

Translation: Users come for the microdrama hook, but the ones who stay engaged shift to feature films. Microdrama gets them in the door. Long-format keeps them around.

Strategic Implications

1. Cross-format promotion is validated. The migration pattern exists organically — we just need to accelerate it. Surface long-format recommendations immediately after microdrama completion.

2. Dec/Jan retention gap wasn't "microdrama users churn more." It was "we didn't cross-promote them to long-form fast enough." Acquisition scaled but the bridge didn't.

3. Samsung TV partnership (microdrama-heavy) needs cross-format sequencing. If MD-first users naturally migrate to long-form, ensure the Samsung library has both.

Data-driven content commissioning rules. The evidence is unambiguous.

09

Strategic Recommendations

P0 This Week

1. Implement Cross-Format Bridge (VALIDATED)
After every microdrama completion, surface a long-format recommendation. The content migration pattern (84% MD on D0 → 12.3% non-MD on D1) proves users WANT to shift. The 48% vs 40% M0 gap is proven. Force the exposure algorithmically — end cards, "watch next" rails, first-session sequencing.
2. Hard Duration Cap on Commissions
Maximum 4,800 seconds (80 minutes). No exceptions. Every minute over 80 costs completion rate. If the story needs more time, make it a series.
3. Homepage Placement Review
Verify microdrama widget position post-Feb 19 layout change. If still below-the-fold, A/B test prominence vs current placement. Discovery matters for acquisition.

P1 This Month

4. D1 Re-Engagement Sequence
Push notification at D1 with personalized microdrama + long-format pairing. The trial watch data shows users shift to long-form on D1 — meet them there. Automated, personalized D1 push is highest-leverage CLM intervention.
5. Kill Underperformers
Any show below 40% Hook→Complete: deprioritize in recommendation, don't renew. Reallocate budget to proven archetypes (relationship drama, 1hr Haryanvi).
6. Bhojpuri Quality Audit
Before adding Bhojpuri microdramas, audit existing titles for production quality, pacing, hook structure. Why do Bhojpuri originals underperform Haryanvi localizations? Fix quality before expanding quantity.

P2 This Quarter

7. Microdrama-to-Series Pipeline
Top microdrama concepts (husband-on-sale, crorepati-biwi) should be expanded into long-format series. Microdrama proves demand; series captures retention. This is the natural extension of the Gateway Hypothesis.
8. New KPI: Cross-Format Conversion Rate
Track % of microdrama-only users who watch long-format within 7 days. This is the true measure of microdrama's strategic value — not microdrama retention or microdrama watch time. Current organic rate: ~12% by D1, ~52% by D0 (overlap). Goal: push 12% → 25% via sequencing.
9. Samsung TV Cross-Format Sequencing
Samsung partnership is microdrama-heavy. Ensure the library includes both MD + long-format. Test auto-play sequencing: MD completion → long-format next-up. Measure whether Samsung cohort retention matches the 48% M0 from cross-format exposure.

The Bottom Line

Microdramas = customer acquisition cost reduction.
Long-format = lifetime value.

Every microdrama dollar should be evaluated by its contribution to cross-format conversion and long-format retention. The 48% vs 40% M0 number is the north star. The content migration pattern (84% MD on D0 → 12.3% non-MD on D1) proves the organic shift exists — we just need to accelerate it. The CTR crash was placement, not content. The duration discipline is the commissioning rule. The cross-format bridge is the product priority.
