Market Themes

1. "Arbitrage Trading Business" (Marmik's Framing)

Micro drama = arbitrage between CPMs and client awareness. Spend on UA, book subscriptions immediately, break even in same transaction. Works as long as CPMs < willingness to pay.

HMT reaction: "Brilliant way to put it. I never ever thought about it in that way."

2. China Content Supply Drying Up

  • Most licensable content already taken
  • Govt censorship limits genres: no horror, no extreme violence, no gore
  • Allowed: comedy, action, limited thrillers
  • Remaining content distributed by DramaShots, RealShots etc.
  • Implication for STAGE: Original regional content is increasingly defensible as licensed Chinese content pool shrinks

3. Industry-Wide Retention Problem

  • Everyone at 3-4% D30 (Sensor Tower) except Pocket TV
  • No one shares real retention numbers voluntarily
  • STAGE advantage: Original content in regional languages = higher emotional engagement = potentially better retention

4. Profitability vs Growth Tradeoff

  • Cuckoo chose profitability (cut UA → ₹22 Cr profit)
  • EloElo chose growth (₹75 Cr marketing spend) but can't raise
  • Vyraloka chose cash flow neutrality
  • STAGE's position: Original content moat allows different unit economics than pure-licensed plays
