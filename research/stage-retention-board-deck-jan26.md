## Customer Lifecycle Management (CLM) Framework

### User Segments

| Segment | Definition | User Base |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **M0** | First 30 days of subscription | 195K |
| **≥M1 Watchers** | ≥31 days sub age + watched in last 4 weeks | 213K |
| **≥M1 Dormants** | ≥31 days sub age + NOT watched in last 4 weeks | 1,170K |

**Note:** 1.17M dormants = ~74% of active subscriber base is dormant. This is the elephant.

### M0 Strategy: Weekly Activity Bucket Transitions
- P1 (0-1 watch days) → P2 (2-4) → P3 (5-7) → Habit (8+)
- Segmented by week: W1 (trial behavior), W2-W4 (post-sub segmentation)
- **Outcomes:** +25pp Watcher %, +2.75pp Habit %

### M1+ Watcher Strategy: Recency-Based Segmentation
- Watched in last week: 93.2K (1st time, repeat, resurrected)
- Inactive in last week: 108K (1-week inactive, 2-3 weeks inactive)
- **Outcomes:** +5pp WAU %, +18pp WoW Retention %

### Dormant Strategy: Two-Tier Segmentation
**Never Watched (711K):** Segmented by recency (31-90d, 91-180d, 180+)
**Previously Watched (471K):** RFM modelling (HVU, MVU, LVU)

**Outcomes:**
- Never Watched Resurrection: 11.7K (Mar) → 32.7K (Dec) = **+180%**
- Prev Watched Resurrection: 20.3K (Mar) → 49.3K (Dec) = **+143%**

### ML Scoring System
**User Intent Score:** Historical behavior signals — ML model predicts likelihood of target behavior
- Tiers: Super High, High, Medium, Low

**Current Status Score:** Real-time probability assessment — updates daily
- Tiers: On Track (P≥20%), Off Track (5-20%), Left Behind (<5%)

**Segmentation Matrix:** Intent × Status → determines intervention intensity
- Super High + Off Track = intervention opportunity (⚠)
- Low + Left Behind = deprioritize

**High Intent Strategy:** Discounted plans at renewal for Super High/High intent users — 4x habit achievement vs others.

## Reflection & Key Takeaways

### What's Working Exceptionally
1. **M1 retention at 53% — #3 in India behind only JioHotstar and Netflix.** This is remarkable for a dialect-first platform. Content-market fit is proven for first-month engagement.
2. **CLM framework is sophisticated.** ML-based intent scoring + status scoring + matrix segmentation is enterprise-grade. Resurrection numbers (180% improvement) validate the approach.
3. **Cross-format consumption is the biggest retention lever.** 48% M0 for dual-format vs 40% single-format. The 8pp lift is massive at scale.
4. **TV users are gold.** 2.4x D30 retention, 5x watchtime. Every TV conversion is worth 2-3 mobile-only users.

### Where the Story Breaks
1. **M1→M3 cliff.** STAGE is #3 at M1 (53%) but drops to #9 at M3 (22%). That's a 31pp drop — steeper than Netflix (13pp) or JioHotstar (12pp). The content library exhaustion or novelty decay hypothesis needs testing.
2. **D1 retention is weak (20%).** STAGE ranks #9 in D1 despite strong M1. This means: users who DO come back at M1 are loyal, but first-day activation is poor. The first session experience is the bottleneck.
3. **1.17M dormants (74% of base).** The CLM dormant resurrection is working (+143-180%) but the absolute base is still massive. At 32.7K/month resurrection from 711K never-watched, that's only 4.6%/month recovery rate.
4. **Microdrama only 15% of library but 20% of title starts.** Demand exceeds supply. The 23 Bhojpuri microdramas vs 44 Haryanvi is a content gap that may partially explain Bhojpuri's weaker engagement.

### Strategic Hypotheses
1. **The M1→M3 cliff is a content depth problem, not a product problem.** Users love what they find (M1 = 53%) but run out of content they care about. Long series weekly drops are the structural fix — Mahapunarjanam proved 40% weekly return through 12 weeks.
2. **D1 weakness + M0 zero-activity = same root cause.** If 65% of M0 subs never engage and D1 is only 20%, the first-hour experience is failing. Auto-play + personalized first session could address both simultaneously.
3. **TV is underinvested relative to impact.** 5.8K WAU with 2.4x D30 lift. If TV users were 10% of base instead of ~1%, the retention curve would shift dramatically. This might be the highest-ROI investment after content.
4. **Cross-format exposure should be P0.** The 8pp M0 lift from dual-format consumption is proven. Every M0 user should be exposed to both formats within their first session. This is a product/algo change, not a content change.
