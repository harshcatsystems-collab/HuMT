## 1. Core Strategic Shift

**From:** Culture-first thinking (make content per regional market)
**To:** Story-first + format-first thinking (great stories that can be productized across cultures)

**Key reframe:** Culture is not the creative core — it's the **distribution superpower**. A great story is great anywhere; culture makes it relatable and personal for each market.

> *"Instead of Hindi, we said every user deserves entertainment in their own culture. So we added a layer of culture, and made the content more personalized."* — HMT

### The "Wrapper" Debate (Major Discussion)

Renu pushed back on calling culture a "wrapper" — arguing culture IS the content (characters, conflicts, stories are born from culture). Extended debate with multiple perspectives:

- **HMT's position:** Culture and story are **equal** but **separable**. The core of filmmaking (character design, plot, production value) is the necessary condition. Culture enables distribution and emotional connection.
- **Renu's position:** Culture gives birth to stories and conflict. Removing culture removes the soul.
- **Nishita's position:** Culture is an emotional enabler. Don't oversimplify for strategy's sake.
- **Resolution:** The word "wrapper" is problematic but the concept stands — culture is both origin and distribution lever. Authenticity is non-negotiable. Reframe the language.

> *"The necessary condition for content to work is not the cultural context. It's that the core of filmmaking should be good."* — HMT

## 2. Why Users Open STAGE (First Principles)

Four reasons a user opens the app at 9 PM with 45 minutes:

| Reason | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Habit** | New episode of a show they follow dropped |
| **FOMO** | Everyone around them is talking about something |
| **Variety** | Confidence that "I'll find something good here" |
| **Exclusivity** | "I can't get this anywhere else" |

---

## 3. North Star Metrics

- **Frequency:** Active days per month → target 12-15 opens/month (3-4x/week)
- **Depth:** Watch time per session → target 25 min/session
- **Combined:** 300-375 minutes per user per month
- **Key insight:** Users who open 4x/week have significantly lower churn

## 4. Content Architecture — Four Layers

| Layer | Purpose | Investment | Examples |
|-------|---------|------------|---------|
| **Layer 1: Habit Engine** | Build daily/weekly viewing habits | **60%** | Long series, episodic content |
| **Layer 2: Variety Platter** | Platform perception — "always something new" | **20%** | Non-fiction, mixed formats |
| **Layer 3: FOMO Engine** | Social buzz, organic acquisition | — | Viral IPs, controversial content |
| **Layer 4: Acquisition Magnet** | Drive awareness and trials | — | Tentpole feature films, events |

### Layer 1 — Long Series (60% of investment)
- Daily/weekly appointment viewing
- Character attachment (after 10 episodes, characters feel like family)
- Low decision cost (users don't choose, they just come)
- Cliffhanger mechanism for return visits
- Community shared pace (group discussions)
- **Mandate:** Character-first storytelling. Every pitch starts with protagonist.

### Layer 2 — Variety (20%)
- Prevents "daily soap app" perception
- Non-fiction emerging as **game-changer** (true crime, reality, cultural IPs)
- In AI world, real human stories become premium
- Production cost 30-50% of scripted
- Cross-culture IPs possible (e.g., cultural competitions)

### Layer 3 — FOMO
- "When 3 friends are watching something and the 4th is not — the 4th subscribes"
- No ad can replicate this
- **STAGE's biggest gap currently**

### Layer 4 — Tentpole Events
- Feature films drive awareness/trials, NOT retention
- Don't need 12 films → need 2-3 **massive tentpole IPs** per year
- Pull 3 months of marketing budget into 1 massive production

## 5. User Segmentation by Behavior

| Segment | Behavior | Need |
|---------|----------|------|
| **Hooked** | Follow 2-3 long series, open 4-5x/week | Continuity — never let their show end without replacement |
| **Browsers** | Open 1-2x/week, watch what looks interesting | Variety — convert by getting them into a long series |
| **Dormant** | Subscribed but barely open | Reactivation event — tentpole film, buzzworthy content, new season |
| **New** | Just subscribed, exploring | Immediate value — 3+ things in first 7 days |

### Critical Moments
- **Most dangerous:** When a long series ends → overlap scheduling, never leave a gap
- **Never end two long series in the same month**
- **New series starts 2 weeks before existing one ends**

## 9. Key Strategic Decisions Made

1. ✅ **Format-first, not culture-first** — budget allocated by format, not by regional market
2. ✅ **All 5 formats retained** — long series, binge, micro drama, feature film, non-fiction
3. ✅ **Content serves two masters** — every piece must solve for retention OR acquisition (or both)
4. ✅ **Stage slate, not culture slate** — one unified slate adopted across cultures
5. ✅ **Stories can originate from any culture** — not forced per-market
6. ✅ **Authenticity non-negotiable** — cultural representation must be honest
7. ✅ **Persona/softer element** — must be central to the slate (who are we creating for?)
