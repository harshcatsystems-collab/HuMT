## Transcript

**Moderator (Rajesh):** Harsh, you are unique. You have created a digital platform which is a true representative of localization, right? You have monetized us — right now you're monetizing Bihar, right? How have you done that? It's an amazing, fascinating story — to be able to... there was an argument India is so hard to monetize in consumer, other than ad, no one paid. But you have shown that you can monetize the mofussil markets of India. So I'd love to know from you — what was your secret sauce?

**HMT:** First of all, hello everyone. Harsh on this side, one of the co-founders of STAGE. We're building an OTT for India's regional cultures.

Moving on to Rajesh's question — I don't think there was any point in the journey where we had a secret sauce. It was a constant process of figuring it out.

When we first got started, we had not set out to build an OTT. We were building a platform for local artists, because we just felt the need that there are a lot of stories which need to be told which are not told.

I remember one of the first set of feedbacks that we started getting post launching the artist-led platform was: **"Hey, thank you for saving our culture."** And we were like... [pause]

So it was actually a lot of iterative building with our users that led us to eventually figure out and crack that — hey, there are so many untold stories. There are so many stories that need a lot more voice. And it just took us in the direction where we said, hey, probably Bollywood and all of the industry put together is not doing a great job at that, and there needs to be a platform that caters to it.

**Moderator:** When we think of digital entertainment, what comes to your mind is Netflix, then maybe Spotify. But I think now we have innovation-led platforms — from STAGE to Pocket FM to Kuku to Pratilipi — which have taken shape in the last 5 years silently, and they have learned to monetize. This has been the most fascinating part of this evolution that has happened. So just take us through your ability to monetize — what is your learning there?

**HMT:** I think two major paradigm shifts played a role.

One was there was a sudden increase in willingness to pay by the audience, thanks to possibly all the shifts that happened during COVID.

Second — the fantastic government initiative called **UPI**, which has actually caused a paradigm shift in how India treats fintech and deals with fintech. And it's been such an inclusive technical infrastructure in this space.

I think both things put together — we were able to unlock the willingness to pay along with the ease of payment. And something like **autopay** effectively made it seamless for our users to just keep consuming.

Apart from that, we were very clear since the inception that we have to create value in the core content proposition. So we realized — what are the things that the users already pay for? For example, if you look at a cable TV subscription, that is something that India has been paying for for a while, right? It sort of began in the '90s, and it's not just an India-alpha phenomenon — it's a nationwide phenomenon. TV penetration goes really deep, it's about 90-95% across the country.

So that was the third insight — people are used to paying for content. It is just that the modern models and the social media age models have started monetizing eyeballs and attention over delivering core value through content.

So — social media, deeper internet penetration, UPI, local pride, local stories — combination of that: STAGE.

And we say — we're creating something where **we want every Indian to take pride in their cultural identity**.

**Moderator:** Excellent.
